## Mechanics Notes

### Overview

- Adventurers are playable landless noble characters who [[mechanics-content/travel|travel]] the world with followers and a Camp, and have a different set of [[mechanics-content/lifestyle|lifestyle]] perks. As they have no steady income from landed [[mechanics-content/titles|titles]], adventurers primarily earn [[mechanics-content/resources|Gold]] by completing [[mechanics-content/adventurer#contracts|contracts]] for landed rulers, and must stock up on Provisions in order to move the camp. Adventurers can always designate heirs and for them this is free.

### Becoming an Adventurer

- There are a number of ways to play as an adventurer:
- Starting as a pre-scripted adventurer in any of the three start dates
- Using the Ruler Designer to create a custom adventurer
- Selecting a landless character with Choose a New Destiny on succession
- If a child far down succession asks to become an adventurer, you may switch to playing as them

### Roll of Patrons

- Adventurers have a Roll of Patrons, which is shown in the Camp menu. Family members, friends, lovers and rulers the adventurer has completed a regular [[mechanics-content/adventurer#contracts|contract]] for, will be added to the Roll of Patrons. Rulers on the Roll of Patrons can be asked for Provisions, [[mechanics-content/resources|Gold]], [[mechanics-content/knight|Knights]], a random [[mechanics-content/army#men-at-arms|men-at-arms regiment]], an arranged marriage, or another contract.

### Adventurer armies

- Adventurers do not have levies to raise, instead relying on [[mechanics-content/army#men-at-arms|men-at-arms]] and [[mechanics-content/knight|knights]]. They do not pay maintenance for their men-at-arms but they also do not reinforce over time. With the Take the Custom Where it Comes [[mechanics-content/lifestyle|lifestyle]] perk, it possible to recruit men-at-arms of the [[mechanics-content/culture|culture]] of your current location.
- When an adventurer successfully sieges a [[mechanics-content/barony|holding]] their [[mechanics-content/army#men-at-arms|men-at-arms]] will be replenished up to half of the holding's loot.
- Adventurers can manually replenish their [[mechanics-content/army#men-at-arms|men-at-arms]] at the cost of [[mechanics-content/resources|Gold]] or Provisions, or via a combined Diplomacy and [[mechanics-content/attributes|Prowess]] challenge.
- In [[mechanics-content/barony|holdings]] with Tribal [[mechanics-content/government|government]] they can also be replenished at the cost of 75 [[mechanics-content/resources|Prestige]] per 25 soldiers for each regiment.
- In [[mechanics-content/barony|holdings]] with Theocracy or Holy Order [[mechanics-content/government|government]] they can also be replenished at the cost of 50 [[mechanics-content/resources|Piety]] per 25 soldiers for each regiment.

### Visiting holdings

- The holding where the Camp is currently located can be visited at any either by hovering over the Camp and selecting Visit Holding or by using the [[mechanics-content/decisions|decision]] with the same name. Once inside you can visit various places. There is no cooldown for visiting a holding but there is for actions taken within. [[mechanics-content/barony|Holdings]] cannot be visited if the adventurer has a disease [[mechanics-content/traits|trait]]. Local castle grounds (only in Castle holdings)
- Can pay levelled [[mechanics-content/resources|Gold]] to increase [[mechanics-content/attributes|Prowess]] by 1, or pay gold to get the Aspiring Blademaster [[mechanics-content/traits|trait]] or gain blademaster experience
- Proving Grounds [[mechanics-content/building|building]] can pay [[mechanics-content/resources|Gold]] to recruit a Master of Arms
- Depending on the follower you brought with you, a chance to increase [[mechanics-content/attributes|Prowess]] by 1. Follower needs high prowess and should be allowed to be a [[mechanics-content/knight|knight]].
- Can pay scaling [[mechanics-content/resources|Gold]] to gain one of two followers

### Gathering provisions

- Adventurers can attempt to gather Provisions every 3 years either by hovering over the Camp and selecting Gather Provisions or by using the [[mechanics-content/decisions|decision]] with the same name. The adventurer can use either its Learning or [[mechanics-content/attributes|Prowess]] to determine the success chance. If the adventurer has a Chief Forager or Huntsman officer it can send them instead, with the success chance depending on the officer's aptitude.

### Camp purpose

- Instead of authority [[mechanics-content/laws|laws]], adventurers set a [[mechanics-content/adventurer#camp-purpose|camp purpose]]. The camp purpose affects which [[mechanics-content/adventurer#contracts|contracts]] are more common, which camp upgrades and special officers are unlocked and what events or event options the adventurers have. It also affects the appearance of characters from that camp. Changing the camp purpose costs 1000 [[mechanics-content/resources|Prestige]].

### Camp temperament

- [[mechanics-content/adventurer#camp-temperament|Camp temperament]] is dictated by the average Opinion of all followers.
- If the average Opinion of followers is 40 or more the [[mechanics-content/adventurer#camp-temperament|camp temperament]] will be
- Steadfast, which gives +0.4% [[mechanics-content/schemes|scheme]] success chance [[mechanics-content/adventurer#contracts|Contract]] Scheme Growth and +5% Toughness per level of fame. If the adventurer has the Absolute Control perk the it will further gain the same amount for every 20 Dread. If the adventurer has the Honored to Serve perk the it will further gain the same amount for every follower, up to a limit of 5.
- Gravitas gives a scaling [[mechanics-content/schemes|scheme]] phase length Personal Scheme Phase Length bonus
- Faithful gives +5 advantage [[mechanics-content/characters#faith-hostility|Faith Hostility]] Advantage

### Contracts

- Adventurers can see [[mechanics-content/adventurer#contracts|Contracts]] on the world map, which are put there by a ruler. Contracts come in 3 tiers. Most contracts require the adventurer to [[mechanics-content/travel|travel]] to their [[mechanics-content/barony|barony]] before accepting them. Rulers will only put up contracts if they have [[mechanics-content/resources|Gold]] to spare.
- Dukes offer tier 2 [[mechanics-content/adventurer#contracts|contracts]] and only to adventurers with the Illustrious level of fame or above
- Kings offer tier 3 [[mechanics-content/adventurer#contracts|contracts]] and only to adventurers with the Exalted Among Men level of fame or above
- Emperors and [[mechanics-content/faith|heads of faith]] offer tier 3 [[mechanics-content/adventurer#contracts|contracts]] and only to adventurers with the the living legend Legendary Figure level of fame
- Rivals and rulers at [[mechanics-content/warfare|war]] with will never put up [[mechanics-content/adventurer#contracts|contracts]]. Additional contracts can be created using the Visit [[mechanics-content/barony|Holding]] [[mechanics-content/decisions|decision]] and speaking with the town crier. Contracts cannot appear in Sahara unless the adventurer's [[mechanics-content/culture|culture]] has the Saharan Nomads [[mechanics-content/traditions|tradition]].

### Regular contracts

- Regular [[mechanics-content/adventurer#contracts|contracts]] are taken for the contractor and can have the following outcomes:
- Success gives +30 Opinion with the contractor, scaled [[mechanics-content/resources|Gold]], and adds the contractor to the Roll of Patrons
- Exceptional Success gives +60 Opinion with the contractor, more scaled [[mechanics-content/resources|Gold]], a Weak [[mechanics-content/hooks|Hook]] on the contractor, and adds the contractor to the Roll of Patrons
- Failure gives -30 Opinion with the contractor and -25 [[mechanics-content/resources|Prestige]]
- The [[mechanics-content/resources|Gold]] reward scales with the [[mechanics-content/adventurer#contracts|Contract]] Tier. Rulers with high Greed will pay less than those with low Greed.

### Justicar contracts

- Justicar [[mechanics-content/adventurer#contracts|contracts]] will make progress towards unlocking the The [[mechanics-content/knight|Knight]] of the Swan [[mechanics-content/decisions|decision]]. If the adventurer already took the decision they will instead grant experience for the Knight-Errant [[mechanics-content/traits|trait]].

### Transport contracts

- Transport [[mechanics-content/adventurer#contracts|contracts]] require adventurers to [[mechanics-content/travel|travel]] to a distant [[mechanics-content/county|county]]. You will have an option to move the camp to the destination to save on a return trip. All of them give a Weak [[mechanics-content/hooks|Hook]] on the contractor.

### Criminal contracts

- Criminal [[mechanics-content/adventurer#contracts|contracts]] have a red border on the map. Both completing or failing a criminal contract will give the Gallowsbait [[mechanics-content/traits|trait]] and add experience for it, with successful contracts giving twice more experience. Criminal contracts can be divided into two kinds depending on whether the contract is offered for or against the contractor.

### Contracts for the contractor

- [[mechanics-content/adventurer#contracts|Contracts]] taken for the contractor will give +30 Opinion with the contractor, add a Weak [[mechanics-content/hooks|Hook]] on them and add them to the Roll of Patrons.

### Contracts against the contractor

- [[mechanics-content/adventurer#contracts|Contracts]] taken against the contractor will give -60 Opinion with them on success.

### Treasure Map

- The Treasure Map [[mechanics-content/adventurer#contracts|contract]] has a very small chance to appear as a purchase option when visiting the town crier. If accepted the contract unlocks the Excavate Treasure [[mechanics-content/decisions|decision]]. Taking the decision sets a destination and brings the option to [[mechanics-content/travel|travel]] either alone or with the camp. Once the destination [[mechanics-content/barony|barony]] is reached it will grant 30 [[mechanics-content/resources|Stress]] Loss and bring the following options:
- Finish the [[mechanics-content/adventurer#contracts|contract]] the regular way and gain 50 [[mechanics-content/resources|Gold]], 65 Stress Loss and -500 Provisions
- Finish the [[mechanics-content/adventurer#contracts|contract]] criminally gain 80 Gold, -75 [[mechanics-content/resources|Prestige]] and the Gallowsbait [[mechanics-content/traits|trait]] and experience for it
- The [[mechanics-content/adventurer#contracts|contract]] does not add anyone to the Roll of Patrons.

### Legitimist Support

- Legitimist Support [[mechanics-content/adventurer#contracts|contracts]] can only appear for adventurers who use the Legitimists [[mechanics-content/adventurer#camp-purpose|camp purpose]] and can also be created via the Request Legitimist Support interaction with Kings and Emperors. Once the contract is accepted the character can negotiate up to 1800 [[mechanics-content/resources|Gold]] and 3000 [[mechanics-content/army#men-at-arms|Men-at-Arms]]. The negotiation starts with 4 events, which can be increased by the following:
- +2 if level of fame is Exalted Among Men or The Living Legend
- +1 if level of devotion is Devoted Servant and contractor has the same [[mechanics-content/faith|faith]]
- +2 if level of devotion is Paragon of Virtue or Religious Icon and contractor has the same [[mechanics-content/faith|faith]]
- +3 if level of splendor is Well-known or above

### Becoming landed

- Adventurers who becomes landed will gain the adventurer Former Adventurer [[mechanics-content/traits|trait]], all followers will become courtiers and gain the Follower trait, and their camp will be demolished with partial refunds for the cost of [[mechanics-content/building|buildings]]. There are four ways in which an adventurer can become landed:
- They can inherit [[mechanics-content/titles|titles]] unless they have the Gallivanter [[mechanics-content/traits|trait]].
- They can take over a realm via the Seize Realm [[mechanics-content/schemes|scheme]].
- They can conquer a realm via the Adventurer Invasion [[mechanics-content/casus-belli|casus belli]]. They can also request rulers to provide them with soldiers and/or join the [[mechanics-content/warfare|war]] against a King or Emperor but if successful the adventurer will become a vassal of the sponsor and it blocks [[mechanics-content/titles|titles]] equal or higher than the sponsor's primary title. Only one ruler can support an adventurer at at time.
- If the Khans of the Steppe DLC is installed they can move their camp to a [[mechanics-content/county|county]] with Herder [[mechanics-content/government|government]] and either use the Adopt the Nomadic Ways [[mechanics-content/decisions|decision]] or the Demand Pastureland interaction. The former costs 750 [[mechanics-content/resources|Prestige]]. The latter costs 75 Prestige, 25 Control and 25 Opinion with the herder's suzerain.
