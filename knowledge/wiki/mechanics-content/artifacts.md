## Mechanics Notes

### Overview

- Artifacts are objects unique in the world that give various bonuses to the character using them and are always inherited by the primary heir. Artifacts are divided into two categories: inventory artifacts and [[mechanics-content/court|court]] artifacts.
- Inventory artifacts are worn by a character via their inventory. Each character can wear up to 9 artifacts: a crown, a journal, a weapon, an armor, a regalia and 4 trinkets.
- [[mechanics-content/court|Court]] artifacts are displayed in a character's [[mechanics-content/royal-court|Royal Court]]. Each court can display up to 15 artifacts: 3 large wall ornaments, 3 small wall ornaments, 2 pieces of furniture, 2 book items, 4 pedestal items, and a throne.
- Artifacts may be given as gifts to other characters. This will grant +20 Opinion if the receiving character can use the artifact and +5 Opinion if it cannot. Inventory artifacts that are not worn as well as [[mechanics-content/court|court]] artifacts can be damaged or lost if the realm capital is besieged. If an artifact is lost during a siege its previous owner will gain a claim on it.

### Rarity

- All artifacts have a Rarity, which determines the artifact's Durability, cost to repair and how much [[mechanics-content/resources|Gold]] is obtained if the artifact is manually destroyed. All artifacts have a Durability, which represents the integrity of the artifact. When it reaches zero, the artifact is destroyed. If a non-historical artifact is inherited by someone who is not a close family member, it will also lose Durability.

### Creating artifacts

- For rulers the main way of obtaining artifacts is via the Commission Artifact [[mechanics-content/decisions|decision]], which allows them to choose what type of artifact they want created and costs 100 [[mechanics-content/resources|Gold]]. While an artifact is created various events can take place, allowing the ruler to pay further to produce a higher rarity artifact. Pedestal Item artifacts cannot be created this way.
- When a pet dies there will be the option to pay 10-20 [[mechanics-content/resources|Gold]] to gain a small wall ornament.
- Book artifacts can be obtained via the activitiesUniversity Visit [[mechanics-content/activity|activity]].
- If a character wins a battle against a Noble opponent there is a chance to gain a Warbanner large wall ornament. Alternatively the artifact can be destroyed to gain 75 [[mechanics-content/resources|Prestige]].

### Hunt trophies

- Successful Hunt [[mechanics-content/activity|activities]] will grant trinket artifacts. If the character has a [[mechanics-content/royal-court|Royal Court]] and the activity variant was not Falconry, the artifacts can also be wall ornaments. These artifacts will always give monthly [[mechanics-content/resources|Prestige]] and depending on their rarity will also have additional effects.
- Foxes, gazelles, hares, roes and non-exotic birds will always be Common
- Hyenas, lynxes, wolves and exotic birds have a 97% chance to be Common and a 3% chance to be Masterwork
- Antelopes, aurochs, bisons, reindeers and stags have a 50% chance to be Common and a 50% chance to be Masterwork
- Bears, leopards, lions and tigers have an 85% chance to be Masterwork and a 15% chance to be Famed

### Inspirations

- Landless characters occasionally gain an Inspiration, allowing them to work on creating an artifact. Characters older than 13 can sponsor the inspirations with [[mechanics-content/resources|Gold]], with the cost depending on how skilled the inspired character is. Once an inspired character is financed they will start working on the artifact. The artifact's effects will depend on the inspired character's [[mechanics-content/traits|traits]].
- Inspired characters can randomly appear in royal courts. The greater a [[mechanics-content/court|court]]'s Grandeur the higher the chance that an inspired character will appear.
- Followers of [[mechanics-content/adventurer|adventurers]] who have the Inspiration is Everywhere [[mechanics-content/lifestyle|lifestyle]] perk can randomly gain inspirations.
- There are multiple types of Inspiration, which determine what type of artifact will be created. If an inspiration can create more than one type of artifact there's a small chance the sponsor will be allowed to choose what sort of artifact they want created. Completing an Adventure inspiration will grant the sponsored character the [[mechanics-content/adventurer|adventurer]] Veteran Adventurer [[mechanics-content/traits|trait]].

### Historical artifacts

- Historical artifacts have mostly fixed effects. If they are lost or the required [[mechanics-content/titles|title]] does not exist in the chosen start date they may be discovered randomly through Adventure inspirations. If a character obtains an artifact it lacks the requirements to use only the [[mechanics-content/royal-court|Court Grandeur]] bonus will be given. Artifacts that need a certain religion can be used if the [[mechanics-content/faith|faith]] has the syncretism [[mechanics-content/tenets|tenet]] for the required religion.

### Swords of El Cid

- Swords of El Cid are two artifacts that can be obtained in two ways. Only one of each artifact can be created and only after year 1066.
- They can be created through adventure inspirations, in which case they will be Pedestal Items. If Tizona is generated this way it can only be used by [[mechanics-content/culture|cultures]] with Iberian heritage.
- They can be created by Rodrigo Díaz if he wins battles while the
- The Song of El Cid story is active, in which case he will receive the option to keep the artifact or send it to Sancho II.

### Bells of Santiago

- The Bells of Santiago are a trinket historical artifact that can only be used by characters Involved or Interloper in the Iberian Struggle. They have a unique mechanic where if a character who is Christian or Muslim or whose [[mechanics-content/faith|faith]] has the Christian Syncretism or Islamic Syncretism [[mechanics-content/tenets|tenet]] obtains the artifact but its religion cannot use it the artifact will be melted it into their own version of the artifact.

### The Double-Moon Tome

- The Double-Moon Tome is a journal historical artifact that starts in possession of Wallada bint al-Mustakfi in 1066. It starts with the following effects:
- [[mechanics-content/attributes|diplomacy]] [[mechanics-content/lifestyle|lifestyle]] +5% Monthly Diplomacy Lifestyle Experience
- [[mechanics-content/attributes|learning]] [[mechanics-content/lifestyle|lifestyle]] +5% Monthly Learning Lifestyle Experience
- [[mechanics-content/schemes|scheme]] phase length -5 Days Romance Phase Length
- [[mechanics-content/schemes|scheme]] phase length -5 Days Seduce Phase Length

### Adventure artifacts

- Adventure artifacts can only be obtained through Adventure inspirations, with the chance increasing with the inspired character's skill. Most of them can only be found a limited number of times. If Legacy of Persia is also installed artifacts restricted to Persia can be found very rarely as part of [[mechanics-content/travel|travel]] events as well.

### Holy relics

- Holy relics are adventure artifacts restricted to a certain religion. They can also be found outside their historical region in [[mechanics-content/county|counties]] belonging to that religion but the chance is 75% lower. If a character has a holy relic from a different religion then all characters of that religion will gain a [[mechanics-content/casus-belli|casus belli]] to recover the relics. Buying a claim of a holy relic costs Piety instead of [[mechanics-content/resources|Prestige]].
