## Mechanics Notes

### Overview

- A Barony is the lowest rank of landed titles and the only rank that is not playable (although they may be held by playable characters). Each county has 1-7 baronies, which will always be in the same realm as the county they're part of. Baronies may be sparsely populated or have a holding.

### Capital

- A barony can be three types of capital: The county capital is the primary barony of a county. Its holding can never be destroyed and is always owned by the ruler who holds the county title. County capitals allow the construction of an additional building and have +1 Fort Level. The county capital can be changed when the owner of the county also has another holding in the county.

### Holdings

- Each barony may have a holding, which represents the main settlement inside it and is required to be able to use the barony. Constructing a new holding will create a title for that barony, which may be held or given to another character. Baronies that are not a county capital can be granted to a Courtier;
- Constructing a Castle, City or Temple holding requires 400 gold. If the county does not have a set of 1 Castle, 1 City and 1 Temple the missing holdings must be constructed first.
- Upgrading a Castle, City, Temple or Temple Citadel holding to level 2 requires 550 gold.
- Upgrading a Castle, City, Temple or Temple Citadel holding to level 3 requires 700 gold.
- Upgrading a Castle, City, Temple or Temple Citadel holding to level 4 requires 850 gold.

### Castle

- Castle holdings can be held directly by any government type except Nomadic.

### City

- City holdings can be held directly by Republic, Holy Order, governor efficiency Administrative, Celestial, Meritocratic, Meritocratic Khanate and Ritsuryō governments.

### Temple

- Which governments can hold directly a temple holding depends on the Clerical Tradition doctrine of the faith of the ruler holding the barony's country title.
- Theocracy government can directly hold temples
- If the doctrine is Lay Clergy any government can directly hold temples
- Constructing a Temple holding will raise Fervor by 1 and grant its county title holder 500 Piety regardless of which ruler funded it. If the Legends of the Dead DLC is installed the ruler who funded the holding has a 10% chance to gain a Holy legend seed.

### Tribe

- Tribe holdings can only be held directly or upgraded by rulers with Tribal or Wanua government. Tribe holdings cannot be constructed. Upgrading a Tribe holding costs 200 Gold and 400 Prestige and requires the planned assemblies Plenary Assemblies innovation. If the Khans of the Steppe DLC is installed they will also give +35% County Fertility Growth.

### Temple Citadel

- Temple Citadel holdings can be held directly and upgraded by any government type except Nomadic but they can only be constructed by rulers with Mandala government.

### Nomadic Settlement

- Nomadic Settlement is a holding type found throughout the Steppe region and will only provide their effects to rulers with Nomadic government. Buildings cannot be constructed in a Nomadic Settlement. A Nomadic Settlement has the following effects:
- Fort Level depends on the Nomadic Capital of their holder
- Nomadic Settlements can be abandoned to a Herder at any point, turning them into Herder Pens.

### Herder Pens

- Herder Pens is a holding type present in all counties controlled by a character with Herder government. Its only effects are giving the county +25 Plague Resistance and -8 Danger. They will also give +2 County Fertility Growth if the county's culture has the Pastoralists or Wolves of the Deep Steppe traditions.

### Razing holdings

- Rulers with Meritocratic Khanate government can raze City, Temple and Tribe holdings held directly every 5 years to gain scaled Gold and rulers with Nomadic government can raze Castle holdings as well. Razing a holding downgrades all buildings by one level, removes them if they can't be downgraded, and if there are no buildings left razing the holding will turn it into a Nomadic Settlement.

### Terrain

- Each Barony has a dominant terrain. The terrain determines the movement speed and combat width in the Barony and can affect the supply limit and development growth in its County as well as which buildings can be constructed.

### Winter

- All baronies that are not in the lower third of the map will suffer winter effects each year. Winter starts near the middle of October and its Severity slowly increases but it is not the same every year. How early and severely it affects a barony depends on its position on the map, with northmost and mountain baronies being affected first and most severely. Winters end at the beginning of March.

### Plagues

- A plague is an outbreak of a contagious disease trait that spreads across the map. If a plague is present within a ruler's domain they unlock the Isolate Capital and Enter Seclusion decisions (these increase the Plague Resistance at a cost of other penalties). If a plague is present within the player's realm or the realm of a neighbor then a button will appear in the lower-right corner, showing all plagues.
- If Plague Resistance is above 10 plagues are 25% less likely to spread to or increase Infection Rate in the barony.
- If Plague Resistance is above 50 plagues are 50% less likely to spread to or increase Infection Rate in the barony.
- If Plague Resistance is above 80 plagues are 75% less likely to spread to or increase Infection Rate in the barony.
- The number of buildings increase the chance for a plague to appear or spread in a barony, especially tradeports, markets, guilds, or if the holding is a city. Plagues spread faster across coastal regions. They are less likely to spread to islands, with the exception of Great Britain.
