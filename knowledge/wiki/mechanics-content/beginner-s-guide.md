## Mechanics Notes

### Overview

- Welcome to the beginner's guide for Crusader Kings III, a grand strategy game where you lead your medieval [[mechanics-content/dynasty|dynasty]] through [[mechanics-content/warfare|warfare]], [[mechanics-content/attributes|diplomacy]] and intrigue on a path to glory. Whether you're a returning player from a previous Crusader Kings [[mechanics-content/titles|title]] or a first time player of the franchise, this guide will give you a head-start in playing and succeeding ingame.

### Selecting a character

- In CKIII you follow the lives of a [[mechanics-content/dynasty|dynasty]] of rulers, rather than a country itself. To begin, click New Game to choose from a selection of [[mechanics-content/interesting-characters|interesting characters]] in either 867 or 1066. Different interesting characters from the two time periods can be selected from the bookmarks along the top of the screen.
- Petty King Murchad of Munster, who rules a small but strong realm in southern Ireland in which you can learn the rules of conquest as you restore your grandfather's kingdom. This start is easier because you are mostly left to yourself on Ireland, with your own autonomous Irish church and little interference from outside the island. If you choose to play the tutorial, you will be assigned this character.
- Duke Vratislav of Bohemia, who rules the Czech people as a vassal of the Holy Roman Empire; once he has accumulated enough wealth, he is ready to crown himself King of Bohemia. Bohemia uses the seniority succession method, which lowers the risk of your realm being divided amongst squabbling brothers on a ruler's death.
- Duke Robert of Apulia, a Norman conqueror who has established himself in southern Italy. As an independent ruler seeking to establish a kingdom, you have room to expand and grow with the chance to establish strong [[mechanics-content/alliance|alliances]] with your neighbours. However, beware:
- Starting as the head of a large kingdom may be tempting, but often comes with the great responsibility of pleasing and managing dozens of potentially disloyal vassals. You will often find a strong vassal scheming against you early on, leaving you vulnerable to rebellion or attack if you cannot placate them.

### Before unpausing

- On loadup you will have automatically zoomed in to see your own realm. In the case of independent rulers you will see independent realms around you, whereas a vassal will see other vassals in their realm as well as their liege's direct [[mechanics-content/barony|holdings]]. Before you unpause the game, there are critical tasks that you should take care of.

### Choosing a lifestyle

- During the course of their lives, rulers will focus on many different aspects of governance and personal development that will determine how effective their rule is. In the choose a lifestyle menu, shown in a red diamond with three [[mechanics-content/resources|gold]] arrows pointing upwards, you will be prompted to choose from 5 different [[mechanics-content/lifestyle|lifestyles]], each with 3 different lifestyle focuses.

### Marriage

- Many rulers in-game will start unmarried, although others may already have a spouse and children. If you have no other eligible family members of your [[mechanics-content/dynasty|dynasty]] to inherit, getting married will be a crucial early step to avoid a sudden game over. Finding a spouse before you unpause is crucial because many of the best choices will be married off to other rulers very quickly.

### Educating children

- A child tends to develop [[mechanics-content/traits|traits]] that belong to their guardian, especially their education trait, which gives a bonus to experience gained toward a specific [[mechanics-content/lifestyle|lifestyle]]. When your own character educates a child, you can make choices that direct what traits the child develops, but some of these choices may produce [[mechanics-content/resources|stress]] if they are contrary to your character's personality.

### Managing your titles

- As a general rule, always fully control at least the duchy where your Realm Capital is located; doing so helps consolidate your power and avoids offending your vassals who might desire your [[mechanics-content/titles|titles]]. Conversely, if you have domain [[mechanics-content/county|counties]] who are de jure part of your vassals' titles, you might want to make a mental note to eventually dispose of these counties.

### Foreign affairs

- Look around the neighbourhood and the foreign rulers nearby. Check if they have any claims on your (or your vassals') [[mechanics-content/titles|titles]]. Also, see if any marriages (and thus [[mechanics-content/alliance|alliances]]) can be made. If playing as a vassal, should your Liege becomes weakened or distracted by other adventures, you should be prepared to join your Liege as an ally to defend your titles.

### Status of innovations

- In 867, many [[mechanics-content/culture|cultures]] are missing [[mechanics-content/innovation|innovations]], such as [[mechanics-content/casus-belli|Casus Belli]] (for De Jure [[mechanics-content/county|County]] Claims) and Plenary Assemblies (for Limited Crown Authority, vital for Feudal and Clan rulers as this allows them to revoke [[mechanics-content/titles|titles]]). Before unpausing, you'll have to decide if you want to pursue the goal of becoming your Culture's Head and thus be able to pick the Innovation for your culture to be Fascinated by.

### Early game

- After unpausing the game (Space), you can adjust the speed (/), as the default speed of 1 is very slow. Faster speeds allow things to go by quickly in peacetime while slower speeds make managing [[mechanics-content/warfare|wars]] easier. Speed 5 causes game time to pass as quickly as your computer can run the simulation and should only be used to pass a large amount of time quickly.

### Domestic affairs

- In addition to foreign affairs, there are often many small domestic affairs that need handling in the first few days. It is important to ensure that your Realm Priest (if your [[mechanics-content/faith|Faith]] allows for the position) endorses you, which provides a boost to tax and levies. This can be as simple as appointing them to the position of [[mechanics-content/court|court]] physician, or might require you to win their approval with gifts or a sway [[mechanics-content/schemes|scheme]].

### Feudal Contracts

- If playing as a feudal character, do check the obligations of your vassals (as a liege) and your own (as a vassal). As much as possible, try to increase tax contributions of your vassals without incurring tyranny; do this by reducing their levy contributions or by giving them a privilege.

### Becoming Rightful Liege

- Becoming your vassals' Rightful Liege is important to maximise your taxes and levies from them. The Rightful Liege mechanic makes it less economical to expand outside your de jure kingdom or empire as at its worst, you only get half of the taxes and levies. Create duchies which are de jure part of your kingdom or empire and grant them to counts (not dukes) which are not your de jure vassals.

### Deal with Factions as Liege

- Obtain strong hook Strong [[mechanics-content/hooks|Hooks]] (Send Spymaster to Find Secrets (unreliable), fabricate strong hooks (requires [[mechanics-content/attributes|intrigue]] Truth is Relative perk)).
- Increase opinion to over +80 opinion (sway, gifts).
- Transfer 2-tier or lower disloyal vassals to higher-tier loyal vassals.
- Make Faction Member [[mechanics-content/court|Court]] Chaplain if [[mechanics-content/faith|Faith]] permits it (can still join claimant factions).
- Increase Total Soldiers (Increase [[mechanics-content/attributes|Martial]], hire Mercenaries or Holy Order).

### Use Factions as Vassal

- Faction members fight together against their liege.
- Join an already strong claimant Faction to get a weak [[mechanics-content/hooks|hook]] Weak Hook on new liege (can be used to modify the vassal [[mechanics-content/adventurer#contracts|contract]] favorably, although fabricating a hook on liege might be safer and faster).
- Can usurp liege [[mechanics-content/titles|title]] as the claimant (can create claim with the [[mechanics-content/attributes|stewardship]] Meritocracy perk).
- Destroy the liege's main [[mechanics-content/titles|title]] to prevent future reconquest.
- Your liege is surrounded by realms which have [[mechanics-content/faith|Faiths]] which regard your faith (assuming you share the same faith as, or has a similar faith to, your liege) as Hostile or Evil. Becoming independent in such an environment may do more harm than good.

### Preparing for succession

- Managing your realm as your character's primary heir after your character's death can be one of the most perilous moments in the game, so it is important to prepare a smooth transition.
- Note the quirks of your [[mechanics-content/government|government]] type's succession [[mechanics-content/laws|laws]]:
- Feudal succession is dependent on availability of [[mechanics-content/innovation|innovations]] and can be changed at the appropriate crown authority.
- Clan succession is usually some form of partition/ high partition, depending on House Unity. This means that Clan realms will most likely not split upon succession, with only one highest-tier [[mechanics-content/titles|title]] present. Clan [[mechanics-content/government|governments]] must pay an additional 1500 [[mechanics-content/resources|Piety]] to change the realm succession to single heir.
- Tribal succession is locked to confederate partition.

### Authority

- If playing as a feudal liege, remember that you can always lower Crown Authority to ward off liberty factions; the cooldown only applies to increasing Authority.
- If playing as a feudal vassal, remember that you can unilaterally change the feudal [[mechanics-content/adventurer#contracts|contract]] once per character. If you are not a rightful vassal of your liege, you already have lowered obligations; make use of this by increasing your obligation one step in order to secure a right. What right to secure depends on your situation:
- If you are of a different [[mechanics-content/culture|culture]] and/or religion from your liege (and intend to maintain the differences), protection from [[mechanics-content/titles|title]] revocation should be a priority.
- If you plan to expand within your liege's realm and your liege is at level 3 crown authority, consider getting sanctioned [[mechanics-content/warfare|war]] declaration.
- If you wish to increase your income, secure guaranteed [[mechanics-content/council|Council]] Rights and force your liege to accept you as Steward; this gives you various monetary benefits.

### Succession

- Monitor which [[mechanics-content/titles|titles]] will be lost upon succession: Click the "Succession" tab under the "Realm" window.
- If maintaining realm integrity is desired, get out of Confederate Partition as soon as possible: It is the only succession [[mechanics-content/laws|law]] which creates new [[mechanics-content/titles|titles]] upon succession, thus almost guaranteeing a split in your realm unless you restrict the realm to the de jure [[mechanics-content/county|counties]] of your primary duchy or kingdom.
- Tribal realms are locked to Confederate Partition; they must reform to either Clan or Feudal [[mechanics-content/government|government]] before being able to select other succession types.
- Feudal rulers can use title succession [[mechanics-content/laws|laws]] on their primary title to prevent realm fractures; the ruler must not have other [[mechanics-content/titles|titles]] of the same rank as their primary title.
- On the flip side, Confederate Partition will increase dynastic [[mechanics-content/resources|Renown]] gain, due to independent landed dynasts. If this approach is desired, then junior heirs should be given sufficient [[mechanics-content/county|counties]] as their power base, so that they are less likely to fold to factions (or lose their [[mechanics-content/titles|title]] in a civil [[mechanics-content/warfare|war]]).
