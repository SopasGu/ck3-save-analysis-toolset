## Mechanics Notes

### Overview

- Each noble character has a court, which consists of the unlanded characters at a character's court. A court may contain family members, [[mechanics-content/knight|knights]], lovers, and courtiers or followers. Rulers may also have hostages and guests.

### Courtiers and followers

- Courtiers and followers are unlanded characters living at a character's court: they are called courtiers if the liege is a ruler and [[mechanics-content/adventurer|adventurers]] if the liege is an adventurer. They can be appointed as councilors, [[mechanics-content/army|commanders]], [[mechanics-content/knight|knights]] or vassals. If a courtier does not have a court position or minor [[mechanics-content/titles|title]] they have a 2% chance to leave each month. The chance is decreased by −0.

### Hostages

- Hostages are landless children that are a Close Family member of another ruler who sends them to another ruler's court as an assurance of peace or submission. Hostages can be offered, demanded or exchanged both to prevent a [[mechanics-content/warfare|war]] or to help end it. A character having a hostage will be called its Warden and will gain the following rewards:
- [[mechanics-content/resources|prestige]] +0.1 to +1 Monthly Prestige depending on difference in [[mechanics-content/titles|title]] rank
- [[mechanics-content/resources|piety]] +0.1 to +1 Monthly Piety depending on difference in [[mechanics-content/titles|title]] rank if the hostage's [[mechanics-content/faith|faith]] is considered Hostile or Evil
- [[mechanics-content/resources|renown]] +0.25 to +0.5 Monthly Renown depending on the Level of Splendor of the hostage's [[mechanics-content/dynasty|dynasty]] if they are part of a different dynasty
- When the hostage reaches the age of 16 there is a chance to gain a

### Guests

- Guests are unlanded characters that stay at a ruler's court for three years and then have a 5% chance to leave each month. During this time they will interact with other members of the court and may take part in random events. They can be convinced to stay permanently in the realm by being given a seat on the [[mechanics-content/council|council]], a court position or a landed or minor [[mechanics-content/titles|title]], or by using the seduction [[mechanics-content/schemes|scheme]].

### Court positions

- Court positions are specialized roles that can be granted to courtiers and direct vassals and grant increasingly powerful benefits but for rulers cost a monthly salary. Characters who are imprisoned cannot be given court positions with the exception of Executioner. A ruler must pay 200 [[mechanics-content/resources|Prestige]] to fire someone with a court position while an [[mechanics-content/adventurer|adventurer]] must pay 25 Prestige.
- −20% if [[mechanics-content/culture|culture]] has the Esteemed Hospitality [[mechanics-content/traditions|tradition]]
- If the ruler's [[mechanics-content/government|government]] uses the Treasury resource the salary will be paid with it instead of [[mechanics-content/resources|Gold]]. The owner of the kingdom Chrysanthemum Throne [[mechanics-content/titles|title]] cannot be given court positions unless they become landed.

### Aptitude

- The bonuses of a court position depend on the courtier or vassal's aptitude. Aptitude goes through 5 levels, each level requiring 20 points of aptitude: +20 Aptitude for Family members of the same [[mechanics-content/culture|culture]] (Culture has the Family Business [[mechanics-content/traditions|tradition]]). −20 Aptitude for Vassals. −75 Aptitude for Infirm characters (−50 for the Caravan Master, none for the Court Gardener).

### Recruit for a Court Position

- Aside from manually assigning courtiers or guests to a court position, it is possible to recruit new characters both via a button in the Court tab and via a [[mechanics-content/decisions|decision]]. This will bring an event where the ruler can choose between a character with above average Aptitude and one with below average Aptitude. Both options have an upfront [[mechanics-content/resources|Gold]] cost that scales with the character's Aptitude and the ruler's monthly income.

### Common positions

- The following court positions are available to all characters and always join the character's entourage. Rulers must pay a salary but [[mechanics-content/adventurer|adventurers]] do not.

### Ruler positions

- Ruler court positions are available only to rulers.

### Duke positions

- Duke court positions are available to Dukes and above.

### King positions

- King court positions are available to Kings and above.

### Emperor positions

- Emperor court positions are available to Emperors and above. Each position can only be given to one character.

### Hegemon positions

- Hegemon court positions are available to hegemony Hegemons that have Celestial [[mechanics-content/government|government]]. Each position can only be given to one character.

### Nomadic positions

- Nomadic court positions are available to characters with Nomadic [[mechanics-content/government|government]]. Each position can only be given to one character and they cannot be given to vassals.

### Officers

- Officers are available to [[mechanics-content/adventurer|adventurer]] character as opposed to regular court positions. They do not have any salary and with the exceptions of Personal Physician, Caravan Master, Stooge, Bodyguard, Camp Priest and Cultural Emissary every position gives the follower +0.1 Monthly [[mechanics-content/resources|Prestige]].

### Captains

- Captains are officers obtained from upgrading a Proving Grounds [[mechanics-content/building|building]] and improve one type of [[mechanics-content/army#men-at-arms|men-at-arms]]. There are no requirements for who can occupy the position beyond the one position per character limit. Each captain position increases the follower's Opinion by 2. Their aptitude is increased by the following:
- +10 if they have any [[mechanics-content/army|commander]] trait (multiple [[mechanics-content/traits|commander traits]] do not stack)
