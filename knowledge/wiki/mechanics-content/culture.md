## Mechanics Notes

### Overview

- The culture represents the customs and technologies that a character or county uses. Rulers can change a county's culture by ordering their Steward to complete the Promote Culture task. Counties will have lowered popular opinion with their direct Liege if they belong to a different culture and especially heritage.

### Culture head

- Each culture with at least one ruler has a Culture Head. The Culture Head is always the adult character who owns the most counties of said culture but if the Khans of the Steppe DLC is installed for some cultures the Culture Head is the character who has the most Herd unless a ruler without Nomadic government has greater military strength.

### Cultural acceptance

- Cultural Acceptance determines how much Opinion penalty two cultures have towards each other. For every culture the culture with the highest Cultural Acceptance will be selected for Culture Spread, which increases the speed at which progress is gained towards an innovation the culture doesn't know but the other culture does by +40%.
- +10 if both cultures share the same ethos and are located within the same geographic region
- +5 if most counties of both cultures share the same religion
- +10 if most counties of both cultures share the same faith
- +5 if most counties of both cultures have an eastern faith

### Cultural pillars

- Each culture has 5 cultural pillars: Heritage, Language, Aesthetics, Martial Custom and Ethos.
- Heritage represents a culture's origin and prevents creating hybrid cultures if both cultures share it.
- Language represents the language that all characters and counties of a culture speak, and determines the title naming and what the culture's knights are called. If a character converts to a culture with a different language, they will instantly learn it.
- Aesthetics determine the naming practices as well as the appearance of architecture, armies, clothes and coat of arms.
- equal Martial Custom determines what genders can act as Commanders and Knights and requires the Royal Court DLC. Without it the faith's View on Gender doctrine determines it.

### Ethos

- Each culture will have an ethos, which represents the core values, principles and attitude towards life that the culture has. It also determines which court types are available. In the Royal Court DLC, reforming a culture's ethos costs 20000 Prestige.

### Traditions

- Each culture has several traditions, which represent the main customs of a culture and can grant various effects. A culture can have up to five traditions in the tribal era, with every additional era reached granting an additional slot for Traditions. The base cost of adding a tradition is 2000 Prestige, which may be increased by the following:
- +2000 if the culture does not have one of the Tradition's preferred ethos.
- +3000 if the Tradition's optional requirement is not fulfilled. This does not apply to AI Culture Heads.
- In the Royal Court DLC replacing a tradition increases the final cost by +50% and the time it takes to establish it by +25%. The needed time to establish a tradition is dependent on the number of counties with that culture. The fewer counties the culture has, the faster it goes. The maximum time to establish a tradition is 30 years. Many traditions are mutually exclusive with other traditions.

### New cultures

- There are two ways through which a new culture can be formed, both of which cost Prestige.

### Hybrid cultures

- Hybrid cultures gain all innovations known by either culture and will start with 100% Cultural Acceptance with both parents cultures. By default hybrid cultures will be given the name of their parent cultures connected by a hyphen, though some hybrids have custom names by default. Their default color will be a mix of the parent cultures' colors. There are three ways to create a hybrid culture:
- If two cultures meet the Cultural Acceptance threshold and a ruler has counties of both cultures within their realm. Depending on game settings it might also require both cultures to be old enough. The hybrid culture can choose heritage, cultural pillars, traditions and aesthetics from either parent culture.
- If a ruler does not share the culture of their realm capital and the two cultures meet the requirements to create a hybrid culture is a 5% chance each year that the ruler will be given the option to create a hybrid culture. While it is free it does not allow its pillars or traditions to be customized and are chosen at random from the two parent cultures.
- Rulers with Nomadic government can create hybrid cultures via a decision but it does not allow its pillars or traditions to be customized and they are chosen at random from the two parent cultures.
- The base threshold for Cultural Acceptance is 40%, but this can be modified by several factors, including the Traditions of the two cultures, the Way of the Nomad trait, and certain Struggle contexts.

### Divergent cultures

- Divergent cultures can be created at any point. Ethos can be freely changed. Language can be changed as long as a culture that has the target language as a pillar occupies at least 10% of the realm's counties, borders the character's culture, and has at least 25% acceptance of it (each condition can be met by a different culture that has the target language as a pillar).

### List of cultures

- Culture divergences that take place after the start date can be stopped by a player who controls 80% of the counties from the original culture.
- Iberian architecture will have Mediterranean appearance and Iberian fashion will have Continental European appearance if the Fate of Iberia DLC is not installed.
- Iranian architecture will have Arabic appearance, Iranian fashion will have Arabic appearance, and Turkic Fashion will have Mongolian appearance if the Legacy of Persia DLC is not installed.
- Byzantine architecture will have Mediterranean appearance and Byzantine fashion will have Mediterranean appearance if the Roads to Power DLC is not installed. Constantinople will also use a unique appearance if the DLC is installed.
- Southeast Asian architecture will have Indian appearance, Korean fashion will have Chinese appearance and Malay and Southeast Asian fashion will have Indian appearance if the All Under Heaven DLC is not installed.

### Nomadic cultures

- Nomadic cultures are located in nomadic regions. Counties with nomadic cultures will use the most common architecture of the region where they're located. If the Khans of the Steppe DLC is installed all of them will use Herd to determine their culture head and will lose the Horse Lords and Steppe Tolerance traditions.

### North Germanic cultures

- There are four cultures with North Germanic heritage. Their Culture Head will always be the ruler who controls most counties of that culture. Between the years 950 and 990 the Norse culture split will take place. If the game was started in 867 all divergent cultures listed below keep the same traditions as Norse. In later start dates Danish, Norwegian and Swedish have the traditions listed below.

### Ancient cultures

- Ancient cultures are all dead and only exist for the purpose of Legends and historical characters. They can be selected for custom characters but do not start with any Innovation. If they are brought back on the map, their Culture Head will be the ruler who controls most counties of that culture.

### Culture splits

- The following cultures will divide into multiple small cultures once certain time periods are reached. A player character will get the option to cancel the culture split if they controls 80% of the counties with the original culture.

### Sicilian melting pot

- Sicilian culture can emerge in any county within the duchies of Benevento, Capua, Apulia, Salerno, Calabria, or Sicily if the following conditions are met:
- County has Norman, Baranis, Italian, Lombard, or Greek culture
- The current county holder is one of those five cultures, but is NOT the same as the county's culture
- An event can fire on a tri-yearly pulse switching an eligible county's culture to Sicilian. This chance is doubled if the county holder is Norman, and quadrupled if a neighboring county is already Sicilian. Unlike melting pot cultures emerging from a cultural split or created by decision, Sicilian only changes the culture of counties, not characters.

### Harrying of the North

- Harrying of the North is a culture mechanic enabled if Duke William II of Normandy wins his starting war against King Harold II Godwineson of England. If playing as Hereward the Wake it will only happen if you choose to travel to England after William wins the war. Once the Harrying of the North begins, every county belonging to a vassal of King William of Anglo-Saxon culture will gain +25 Control but also -0.
- Harrying of the North has two values to keep track of which are affected by events: Resistance and Pacification. It will start at 5 Resistance and 0 Pacification. Resistance and Pacification come in four levels and whichever value is higher will impart its effects: Harrying of the North can end in three ways. All of them will give the king of England -65 Stress.
- If none of the vassals of the king of England has Anglo-Saxon culture or is a Duke
- If the king of England does not have Norman or English culture
