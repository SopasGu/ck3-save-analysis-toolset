## Mechanics Notes

### Overview

- The culture represents the customs and technologies that a character or county uses. Rulers can change a county's culture by ordering their Steward to complete the Promote Culture task. [[mechanics-content/county|Counties]] will have lowered popular opinion with their direct Liege if they belong to a different culture and especially heritage.

### Culture head

- Each culture with at least one ruler has a [[mechanics-content/culture#culture-head|Culture Head]]. The Culture Head is always the adult character who owns the most [[mechanics-content/county|counties]] of said culture but if the Khans of the Steppe DLC is installed for some cultures the Culture Head is the character who has the most [[mechanics-content/resources|Herd]] unless a ruler without Nomadic [[mechanics-content/government|government]] has greater military strength.

### Cultural acceptance

- [[mechanics-content/culture#cultural-acceptance|Cultural Acceptance]] determines how much Opinion penalty two cultures have towards each other. For every culture the culture with the highest Cultural Acceptance will be selected for Culture Spread, which increases the speed at which progress is gained towards an [[mechanics-content/innovation|innovation]] the culture doesn't know but the other culture does by +40%.
- +10 if both cultures share the same ethos and are located within the same geographic region
- +5 if most [[mechanics-content/county|counties]] of both cultures share the same religion
- +10 if most [[mechanics-content/county|counties]] of both cultures share the same [[mechanics-content/faith|faith]]
- +5 if most [[mechanics-content/county|counties]] of both cultures have an eastern [[mechanics-content/faith|faith]]

### Cultural pillars

- Each culture has 5 cultural pillars: Heritage, Language, Aesthetics, [[mechanics-content/attributes|Martial]] Custom and Ethos.
- Heritage represents a culture's origin and prevents creating [[mechanics-content/culture#hybrid-cultures|hybrid cultures]] if both cultures share it.
- Language represents the language that all characters and [[mechanics-content/county|counties]] of a culture speak, and determines the [[mechanics-content/titles|title]] naming and what the culture's [[mechanics-content/knight|knights]] are called. If a character converts to a culture with a different language, they will instantly learn it.
- Aesthetics determine the naming practices as well as the appearance of architecture, [[mechanics-content/army|armies]], clothes and coat of arms.
- equal [[mechanics-content/attributes|Martial]] Custom determines what genders can act as [[mechanics-content/army|Commanders]] and [[mechanics-content/knight|Knights]] and requires the [[mechanics-content/royal-court|Royal Court]] DLC. Without it the [[mechanics-content/faith|faith]]'s View on Gender [[mechanics-content/doctrines|doctrine]] determines it.

### Ethos

- Each culture will have an ethos, which represents the core values, principles and attitude towards life that the culture has. It also determines which [[mechanics-content/court|court]] types are available. In the [[mechanics-content/royal-court|Royal Court]] DLC, reforming a culture's ethos costs 20000 [[mechanics-content/resources|Prestige]].

### Traditions

- Each culture has several [[mechanics-content/traditions|traditions]], which represent the main customs of a culture and can grant various effects. A culture can have up to five traditions in the tribal era, with every additional era reached granting an additional slot for Traditions. The base cost of adding a tradition is 2000 [[mechanics-content/resources|Prestige]], which may be increased by the following:
- +2000 if the culture does not have one of the [[mechanics-content/traditions|Tradition]]'s preferred ethos.
- +3000 if the [[mechanics-content/traditions|Tradition]]'s optional requirement is not fulfilled. This does not apply to AI Culture Heads.
- In the [[mechanics-content/royal-court|Royal Court]] DLC replacing a tradition increases the final cost by +50% and the time it takes to establish it by +25%. The needed time to establish a tradition is dependent on the number of [[mechanics-content/county|counties]] with that culture. The fewer counties the culture has, the faster it goes. The maximum time to establish a tradition is 30 years. Many [[mechanics-content/traditions|traditions]] are mutually exclusive with other traditions.

### New cultures

- There are two ways through which a new culture can be formed, both of which cost [[mechanics-content/resources|Prestige]].

### Hybrid cultures

- [[mechanics-content/culture#hybrid-cultures|Hybrid cultures]] gain all [[mechanics-content/innovation|innovations]] known by either culture and will start with 100% [[mechanics-content/culture#cultural-acceptance|Cultural Acceptance]] with both parents cultures. By default hybrid cultures will be given the name of their parent cultures connected by a hyphen, though some hybrids have custom names by default. Their default color will be a mix of the parent cultures' colors. There are three ways to create a hybrid culture:
- If two cultures meet the [[mechanics-content/culture#cultural-acceptance|Cultural Acceptance]] threshold and a ruler has [[mechanics-content/county|counties]] of both cultures within their realm. Depending on game settings it might also require both cultures to be old enough. The [[mechanics-content/culture#hybrid-cultures|hybrid culture]] can choose heritage, cultural pillars, [[mechanics-content/traditions|traditions]] and aesthetics from either parent culture.
- If a ruler does not share the culture of their realm capital and the two cultures meet the requirements to create a [[mechanics-content/culture#hybrid-cultures|hybrid culture]] is a 5% chance each year that the ruler will be given the option to create a hybrid culture. While it is free it does not allow its pillars or [[mechanics-content/traditions|traditions]] to be customized and are chosen at random from the two parent cultures.
- Rulers with Nomadic [[mechanics-content/government|government]] can create [[mechanics-content/culture#hybrid-cultures|hybrid cultures]] via a [[mechanics-content/decisions|decision]] but it does not allow its pillars or [[mechanics-content/traditions|traditions]] to be customized and they are chosen at random from the two parent cultures.
- The base threshold for [[mechanics-content/culture#cultural-acceptance|Cultural Acceptance]] is 40%, but this can be modified by several factors, including the [[mechanics-content/traditions|Traditions]] of the two cultures, the Way of the Nomad [[mechanics-content/traits|trait]], and certain Struggle contexts.

### Divergent cultures

- [[mechanics-content/culture#divergent-cultures|Divergent cultures]] can be created at any point. Ethos can be freely changed. Language can be changed as long as a culture that has the target language as a pillar occupies at least 10% of the realm's [[mechanics-content/county|counties]], borders the character's culture, and has at least 25% acceptance of it (each condition can be met by a different culture that has the target language as a pillar).

### List of cultures

- Culture divergences that take place after the start date can be stopped by a player who controls 80% of the [[mechanics-content/county|counties]] from the original culture.
- Iberian architecture will have Mediterranean appearance and Iberian fashion will have Continental European appearance if the Fate of Iberia DLC is not installed.
- Iranian architecture will have Arabic appearance, Iranian fashion will have Arabic appearance, and Turkic Fashion will have Mongolian appearance if the Legacy of Persia DLC is not installed.
- Byzantine architecture will have Mediterranean appearance and Byzantine fashion will have Mediterranean appearance if the Roads to Power DLC is not installed. Constantinople will also use a unique appearance if the DLC is installed.
- Southeast Asian architecture will have Indian appearance, Korean fashion will have Chinese appearance and Malay and Southeast Asian fashion will have Indian appearance if the All Under Heaven DLC is not installed.

### Nomadic cultures

- Nomadic cultures are located in nomadic regions. [[mechanics-content/county|Counties]] with nomadic cultures will use the most common architecture of the region where they're located. If the Khans of the Steppe DLC is installed all of them will use [[mechanics-content/resources|Herd]] to determine their [[mechanics-content/culture#culture-head|culture head]] and will lose the Horse Lords and Steppe Tolerance [[mechanics-content/traditions|traditions]].

### North Germanic cultures

- There are four cultures with North Germanic heritage. Their [[mechanics-content/culture#culture-head|Culture Head]] will always be the ruler who controls most [[mechanics-content/county|counties]] of that culture. Between the years 950 and 990 the Norse culture split will take place. If the game was started in 867 all [[mechanics-content/culture#divergent-cultures|divergent cultures]] listed below keep the same [[mechanics-content/traditions|traditions]] as Norse. In later start dates Danish, Norwegian and Swedish have the traditions listed below.

### Ancient cultures

- Ancient cultures are all dead and only exist for the purpose of Legends and historical characters. They can be selected for custom characters but do not start with any [[mechanics-content/innovation|Innovation]]. If they are brought back on the map, their [[mechanics-content/culture#culture-head|Culture Head]] will be the ruler who controls most [[mechanics-content/county|counties]] of that culture.

### Culture splits

- The following cultures will divide into multiple small cultures once certain time periods are reached. A player character will get the option to cancel the culture split if they controls 80% of the [[mechanics-content/county|counties]] with the original culture.

### Sicilian melting pot

- Sicilian culture can emerge in any [[mechanics-content/county|county]] within the duchies of Benevento, Capua, Apulia, Salerno, Calabria, or Sicily if the following conditions are met:
- [[mechanics-content/county|County]] has Norman, Baranis, Italian, Lombard, or Greek culture
- The current [[mechanics-content/county|county]] holder is one of those five cultures, but is NOT the same as the county's culture
- An event can fire on a tri-yearly pulse switching an eligible county's culture to Sicilian. This chance is doubled if the county holder is Norman, and quadrupled if a neighboring county is already Sicilian. Unlike melting pot cultures emerging from a cultural split or created by [[mechanics-content/decisions|decision]], Sicilian only changes the culture of [[mechanics-content/county|counties]], not characters.

### Harrying of the North

- Harrying of the North is a culture mechanic enabled if Duke William II of Normandy wins his starting [[mechanics-content/warfare|war]] against King Harold II Godwineson of England. If playing as Hereward the Wake it will only happen if you choose to [[mechanics-content/travel|travel]] to England after William wins the war. Once the Harrying of the North begins, every [[mechanics-content/county|county]] belonging to a vassal of King William of Anglo-Saxon culture will gain +25 Control but also -0.
- Harrying of the North has two values to keep track of which are affected by events: Resistance and Pacification. It will start at 5 Resistance and 0 Pacification. Resistance and Pacification come in four levels and whichever value is higher will impart its effects: Harrying of the North can end in three ways. All of them will give the king of England -65 [[mechanics-content/resources|Stress]].
- If none of the vassals of the king of England has Anglo-Saxon culture or is a Duke
- If the king of England does not have Norman or English culture
