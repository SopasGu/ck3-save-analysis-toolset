## Mechanics Notes

### Overview

- A Dynasty represents an unbroken noble line descending from the same Landed character who founded the dynasty. Each dynasty is made up of multiple Houses working towards bringing [[mechanics-content/resources|Renown]] upon the dynasty. A dynasty can be renamed by the Dynasty Head at any point.

### Houses

- Each dynasty will have a Founding House, usually of the same name as the dynasty, which is the first house of that dynasty. Ruling members of a dynasty may choose to create a Cadet Branch, a new house under the dynasty. Each house has its own name and motto, usually inspired by the location in which they are founded and the founding character, which can be changed at will by the House Head.

### House head

- The House Head is the leader of a house. House Heads have the power to call house members to [[mechanics-content/warfare|war]] at no cost and demand that they convert to their [[mechanics-content/faith|Faith]] and get a Weak [[mechanics-content/hooks|Hook]] on all newly born house members and monthly [[mechanics-content/resources|prestige]] from living and landed members of their house.

### Dynasty head

- The most powerful House Head of a dynasty will be the Dynasty Head. If another House Head becomes 10% stronger than the current Dynasty Head it will take its place. Most of the time Dynasty Head is calculated by military strength, but if all House Heads have governor efficiency Administrative, Celestial, Meritocratic, Meritocratic Khanate or Ritsuryō [[mechanics-content/government|government]] then it will be calculated by powerful family rating.

### Adoption

- Adoption is available to rulers who are unmarried or in a marriage that does not bring children into their House and allows them to adopt a child character into their House. It requires one of the following but can also be permanently disabled or enabled in [[mechanics-content/game-rules|game rules]]:
- [[mechanics-content/culture|Culture]] has the Noble Adoption, Cultivated Sophistication or Dynastic Pragmatism [[mechanics-content/traditions|tradition]]
- Spouse has the same sex and there is no opposite gender Consort or Concubine
- Children cannot be adopted if they are imprisoned, they or close or extended family members are landed or they are already part of the same house. Unless the [[mechanics-content/culture|culture]] has the Noble Adoption or Dynastic Pragmatism [[mechanics-content/traditions|tradition]] or the Adoption game rule is set to Always Allowed, adoption costs 150 [[mechanics-content/resources|Prestige]] and 75 Renown and may also cost Piety depending on the child's [[mechanics-content/faith|faith]]:
- Acceptance is increased by having no parents, friendship, guardianship, shared personality [[mechanics-content/traits|traits]] or if the child has the Ambitious trait. Acceptance can be decreased by having parents or being already part of a house. The child's Opinion of both the adopter and their parents affects acceptance as well. Influence can also be spent to increase acceptance if the [[mechanics-content/government|government]] uses the resource.

### Blocs

- Blocs are defensive [[mechanics-content/alliance|alliances]] between rulers from different houses present in Japan and also block members from going to [[mechanics-content/warfare|war]] against each other. Only house heads can leave blocs or ask to join another bloc. If a house leaves a bloc all its members will gain a 5-year truce will all members of houses from their previous bloc.

### Disinheriting

- Disinheriting is a character interaction that will give the targeted character the Disinherited [[mechanics-content/traits|trait]] and a permanent -75 Opinion. It can be reversed at any point at the cost of 75 [[mechanics-content/resources|Renown]]. Disinheriting requires the target to be a vassal and is not available towards characters with a Strong [[mechanics-content/hooks|Hook]]. Reversing a disinheritance costs 75 Renown.
- Free if the targeted dynasty member has the Disputed Heritage or Bastard [[mechanics-content/traits|trait]].
- 75 Renown and 150 [[mechanics-content/resources|Prestige]] if the targeted dynasty member is Unlanded
- 150 Renown and 300 [[mechanics-content/resources|Prestige]] if the targeted dynasty member is Heir to a [[mechanics-content/titles|title]]
- 225 Renown and 900 [[mechanics-content/resources|Prestige]] if the targeted dynasty member is Landed or part of an governor efficiency Administrative family

### Renown

- Renown is a resource accumulated by a dynasty and is used for the most powerful Dynasty Head interactions, unlocking legacies and increasing a dynasty's level of Splendor. Each living dynasty member will add Renown to the dynasty, and [[mechanics-content/resources|Prestige]] to its Dynasty Head and House Head. Being married to a ruler of another dynasty grants 80% of the Renown their primary [[mechanics-content/titles|title]] generates.

### Level of Splendor

- Firstly, all [[mechanics-content/resources|renown]] earned by a dynasty counts towards its Level of Splendor, which determines how well-known the dynasty is in the eyes of the world. Having a high level also makes it much easier to arrange marriages, especially with Dynasties with a Lower Level of Splendor. The Level of Splendor will only decrease if a player character attempts suicide. Peasant Dynasties start at Base Origins.

### Dynasty legacies

- Dynasty legacies are permanent bonuses that can be unlocked by the Dynasty Head and affect every member of a dynasty. Each legacy track contains five legacies. If an AI character becomes Dynasty Head it will only unlock legacies belonging to the track with the most current progress. If the AI selects Architected Ancestry a random [[mechanics-content/traits|trait]] will be chosen.

### North Germanic legacies

- Dynasties whose Dynasty Head has North Germanic heritage or a [[mechanics-content/culture|culture]] descended from Norse have two additional legacy tracks available. Once the first Legacy of a track has been unlocked its track will remain available regardless of the Dynasty Head's culture.

### Iberian legacies

- Dynasties whose Dynasty Head has Iberian heritage or the realm capital in the region of Iberia have two additional legacy tracks available. Once the first Legacy of a track has been unlocked the track will remain available regardless of Dynasty Head.

### Brilliance

- Dynasties whose Dynasty Head has Iranian heritage or is descended from any [[mechanics-content/culture|culture]] with it have the Brilliance legacy track available. Once the first Legacy has been unlocked the track will remain available regardless of the Dynasty Head's culture.

### Bureaucracy

- Dynasties whose Dynasty Head has governor efficiency Administrative [[mechanics-content/government|government]] have the Bureaucracy legacy track available.

### Nomadic

- Dynasties whose Dynasty Head has Nomadic [[mechanics-content/government|government]] have the Nomadic legacy track available.

### Virtues

- Dynasties whose Dynasty Head has Celestial, Meritocratic or Meritocratic Khanate [[mechanics-content/government|government]] have the Virtues legacy track available.

### Divine Aspirations

- Dynasties whose Dynasty Head has Mandala or Wanua [[mechanics-content/government|government]] have the Divine Aspirations legacy track available.
