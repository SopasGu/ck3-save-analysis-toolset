## Mechanics Notes

### Overview

- Each character will follow a faith, a specific denomination of one religion which dictates how a character should live.

### Fervor

- Fervor is a measure of how righteous adherents believe that a faith is and ranges from 0% to 100%. Fervor determines how expensive it is for rulers to create new faiths and how likely [[mechanics-content/county|counties]] and characters are to convert. Fervor increases at a rate of +3.5 each year, modified by the virtues and sins of religious leaders, particularly the head of faith.

### Faith hostility

- Each faith has a view of every other faith that depends on their Theism [[mechanics-content/doctrines|doctrines]], whether they have any syncretism [[mechanics-content/tenets|tenets]], and whether they have any religion-specific doctrines. The Pluralist doctrine halves the penalties while the Fundamentalist doctrine doubles them. [[mechanics-content/warfare|Holy wars]] and intermarriages will end if one of the characters converts to a faith that no longer allows them.

### Faith organization

- Faith organization describes how organized and coherent each faith is. Faiths can be divided into organized and unreformed, each having advantages and drawbacks. Most Pagan faiths start unreformed and all other faiths except Kuzarism start organized.

### Virtues and sins

- Virtues and sins are [[mechanics-content/traits|traits]] which a religion or faith considers virtuous or sinful. Characters receive +10 Same Faith Opinion and +1 Monthly [[mechanics-content/resources|Piety]] for each virtue trait they have and they lose -10 Same Faith Opinion and -1 Monthly Piety for each sin trait they have. If a trait is considered a virtue by both the faith's religion and one or more of the faith's [[mechanics-content/tenets|tenets]], the bonuses will stack.

### Head of faith

- The head of faith is the formal leader of a faith. Whether a faith has a head of faith or not, as well as how the head of faith is chosen if there is one, is determined by the [[mechanics-content/doctrines|doctrine]] with the same name. Heads of faith gain +1 [[mechanics-content/resources|Piety]] per month. The head of faith is a [[mechanics-content/titles|title]]. To create it, a character requires control of a [[mechanics-content/holy-sites|Holy Site]], 300 Gold and at least a Devoted level of devotion.

### Conversion

- Each character can convert to a different faith at any point. To do so, click on the Faith button on the bottom left corner of the screen (between the House and [[mechanics-content/culture|Culture]] buttons) and select the desired Faith from the Other Faiths menu. Conversion costs [[mechanics-content/resources|Piety]] based on the difference between the [[mechanics-content/doctrines|doctrines]] of the new faith and the doctrines of the old one. The cost can also be affected by the following:
- −20% if [[mechanics-content/barony|holding]] Stonehenge special [[mechanics-content/building|building]] in Salisbury
- −25% if both old faith and new faith has Pluralistic [[mechanics-content/doctrines|doctrine]]
- −25% if converting to the faith of a concubine
- −50% if converting to an existing faith within the same religion

### New faiths

- A ruler who is at peace can create a new faith for a religion once per lifetime by spending a very large amount of [[mechanics-content/resources|piety]]. The farther the new faith's [[mechanics-content/tenets|tenets]] will be from the character's original faith, the more piety it will cost to change it. Once a new faith is created, the ruler, their close family and their realm capital will instantly convert.

### Reformation

- Faiths that are unreformed can be reformed by a ruler who controls at least 3 [[mechanics-content/holy-sites|holy sites]] by spending a large amount of [[mechanics-content/resources|Piety]]. Upon reformation, the new faith will keep the name of the old faith while the old faith will be renamed. Reforming a faith does not require changing any [[mechanics-content/tenets|tenet]] or [[mechanics-content/doctrines|doctrine]] and AI rulers who reform their faith will never do it except possibly for those regarding the head of faith.
- At least three of the [[mechanics-content/county|counties]] housing the faith's [[mechanics-content/holy-sites|holy sites]] follow the faith
- At least one of the [[mechanics-content/holy-sites|holy sites]] is in domain or belongs to a vassal with the same faith
- At least one other independent ruler follows the same faith, has a [[mechanics-content/holy-sites|holy site]] in their realm, and is not Zealous or Rival
- At leans one independent ruler that is a neighbor or shares a seazone and follows a Hostile or Evil reformed faith from a different religion won a [[mechanics-content/warfare|war]], controls one of the unreformed faith's [[mechanics-content/holy-sites|holy sites]], or controls at least 4 [[mechanics-content/county|counties]] that follow the unreformed faith

### Religion

- Religions have three main effects. First, each religion has a baseline set of sins and virtues. Second, the religion determines what holy [[mechanics-content/building|building]] can be constructed. Third, the religion determines which [[mechanics-content/army#men-at-arms|men-at-arms]] its holy orders will favor. A faith's [[mechanics-content/tenets|tenets]] can add [[mechanics-content/traits|traits]] considered sins and virtues, replacing some of the original ones if they happen to conflict.
