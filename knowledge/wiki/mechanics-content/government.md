## Mechanics Notes

### Overview

- A government is the system that rules characters and their [[mechanics-content/titles|titles]]. It determines the [[mechanics-content/laws|laws]] available to a character as well as any obligations (taxes and levies) a vassal may have to their liege. Governments can only be changed via [[mechanics-content/decisions|decisions]]. Governments are divided into three types:
- Landed governments require the character to own landed [[mechanics-content/titles|titles]]. If a player character loses all their landed titles, they must choose to play as another [[mechanics-content/dynasty|dynasty]] member or receive a game over but if the Roads to Power DLC is installed, they will have the option to choose between doing that or changing government to [[mechanics-content/adventurer|Adventurer]].
- [[mechanics-content/domicile|Domicile]] governments give their house heads or potentially all rulers a domicile, which allows a player to play as them even if they do not own landed [[mechanics-content/titles|titles]]. If a ruler changes their government to a landed one, their domicile is sold for [[mechanics-content/resources|Gold]], all title [[mechanics-content/army#men-at-arms|men-at-arms]] are removed, and if their government used Treasury or Herd they are converted to Gold as well.
- Non-dynastic governments do not use dynastic succession and as a result are not playable. When a character with a non-dynastic government dies another random character will inherit all [[mechanics-content/titles|titles]]. If a character with a non-dynastic government inherits a realm of higher rank they will not change government.

### Tribal

- Tribal governments have access to the Tribal Authority [[mechanics-content/laws|law]], unlock Subjugate and Conquest [[mechanics-content/casus-belli|casus belli]], and can start a Co-Monarchy. All tribal rulers can raid unless their [[mechanics-content/faith|faith]] forbids it and gain 100 more [[mechanics-content/resources|Prestige]] when they win a battle. However, they cannot construct [[mechanics-content/barony|holdings]] or use [[mechanics-content/innovation|innovations]] beyond the Tribal Era.
- Tribal rulers pay for [[mechanics-content/army#men-at-arms|men-at-arms]] using [[mechanics-content/resources|Prestige]] instead of Gold but the maintenance cost is reduced by 30%.
- Tribal rulers cannot imprison vassals or courtiers without passing level 2 Tribal Authority.
- Tribal vassals provide taxes and levies based on the Liege's Level of Fame.
- −20 Opinion with vassals that do not have Tribal, Wanua or Nomadic government

### Feudal

- Feudal governments can create new [[mechanics-content/barony|holdings]] and cadet branches, have access to the Crown Authority law, can apply [[mechanics-content/titles|Title]] Succession [[mechanics-content/laws|laws]] and can start a Co-Monarchy.

### Clan

- Clan governments can create new [[mechanics-content/barony|holdings]] and cadet branches and have access to the Crown Authority [[mechanics-content/laws|law]]. All powerful vassals will want to be in an [[mechanics-content/alliance|Alliance]] with their liege and will gain -30 Opinion of their liege if they are not. When clan rulers are at peace, they can appoint a Vizier.

### Tax Jurisdictions

- Vassals with Clan government provide taxes and levies via Tax Jurisdictions. If a ruler has vassals with Clan government then the Tax Jurisdictions tab will become available on the sidebar. Each Tax Jurisdiction is made up of a Tax Collector and up to 12 Taxpayers. Taxpayers are assigned automatically but can be manually added or removed afterwards.
- +15 if [[mechanics-content/culture|culture]] has the Enlightened Magnates [[mechanics-content/traditions|tradition]]
- +20 if [[mechanics-content/culture|culture]] has the Family Business [[mechanics-content/traditions|tradition]]
- Each Tax Jurisdiction has a Tax Decree, which determines what all vassals assigned to the jurisdiction pay. Each Tax Decree can only be changed once every 5 years and the menu will show current taxes and levies and taxes and levies should the Tax Decree be changed. Existing vassals will be removed as taxpayers if the new Tax Decree does not allow them.

### House unity

- If a House Head has Clan government, then its house will have an additional mechanic called House Unity, representing how well members of a house tend to get along with each other. It will be displayed for all house members but only those who have Clan government will be affected by its effects. House Unity is increased by taking Unifying Actions towards them and decreased by taking Divisive Actions towards them.
- Custom characters replacing any of these houses will inherit their House Unity

### Mandala

- Mandala governments can create new [[mechanics-content/barony|holdings]] and cadet branches and ask rulers to become tributaries willingly, and unlock the Capital Temple Complex and Capital Temple Complex Upgrade [[mechanics-content/great-projects|great projects]] as well as the Khlon Glan and [[mechanics-content/court|Court]] Brahmin court positions. They also have access to the Decree [[mechanics-content/laws|law]] but the only succession available is Mandala Succession.
- Level 2 aspects cost 250 [[mechanics-content/resources|Prestige]] and 1000 Piety and requires the Pilgrim [[mechanics-content/traits|trait]] to have at least 25 experience
- Level 3 aspects cost 500 [[mechanics-content/resources|Prestige]] and 2500 Piety and requires the Pilgrim [[mechanics-content/traits|trait]] to have at least 50 experience
- Level 4 aspects cost 1000 [[mechanics-content/resources|Prestige]] and 5000 Piety and requires the Pilgrim [[mechanics-content/traits|trait]] to have at least 75 experience
- Level 5 aspects cost 2000 [[mechanics-content/resources|Prestige]] and 10000 Piety and requires the Pilgrim [[mechanics-content/traits|trait]] to have maximum experience

### Wanua

- Wanua governments have access to the Tribal Authority [[mechanics-content/laws|law]], unlock Subjugate and Conquest [[mechanics-content/casus-belli|casus belli]], and can start a Co-Monarchy. Wanua rulers can sail in major rivers, can raid unless their [[mechanics-content/faith|faith]] forbids it, and gain 100 more [[mechanics-content/resources|Prestige]] when they win a battle.
- Development only affects the Supply Limit and does not passively grow in [[mechanics-content/county|counties]] ruled by a wanua ruler. Wanua [[mechanics-content/barony|baronies]] only provide half of their supply limit to [[mechanics-content/army|armies]] belonging to other governments. Wanua governments have the following unique effects:
- Wanua rulers have a Vassal Limit of 4 regardless of [[mechanics-content/titles|title]] rank.
- Wanua rulers cannot imprison vassals or courtiers without passing level 2 Tribal Authority.
- Wanua vassals provide taxes and levies based on the Liege's Level of Fame.

### Non-dynastic governments

- If a player selects a Herder character to play, government will automatically become Nomadic when the game starts. Realm Priests receive vassal income at a rate of 25% from Realm Priests belonging to their liege's direct vassals. They also provide up to 50% of their income from directly-controlled churches and their vassal priests to their feudal liege, scaled based on opinion.
