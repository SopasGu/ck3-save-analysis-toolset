## Mechanics Notes

### Overview

- This page aims to list interesting characters in the game be it because of the unique challenge they offer, their particular situation, or their role in history. For more interesting starts, check the Crusader Kings 2 interesting characters guide page; most playable characters in 867, 1066 or 1178 on the list are also playable in Crusader Kings III. = 867 start =

### Asturias

- Hermenexildo Gutierrez Menéndez, Count of Santiago: A powerful vassal to king Adelfonso III, Hermenexildo controls the lands of southern Galicia with an unsullied administration. An unquestionable loyalty towards his monarch has helped him expand the ancestral dominion of his family — but is that enough? With the growing interest in the South, will you be able to conquer the lands of Portucale and start one of its...
- Alfonsu III Ordoñes Cantabria, King of Asturias: Alfonsu rose to power during a time of great instability but swiftly consolidated his realm, securing his position through strategic diplomacy. He forged a crucial alliance with the King of Navarra by marrying his daughter, Ximena. From there, he dedicated his reign to battling the Muslims of Al-Andalus, steadily pushing his kingdom’s borders further south.
- Vitiza II Vermundez de Galicia, Count of Monterrei: Vitiza II de Galicia rules the rugged frontier of Galicia under the authority of King Alfonso III of Asturias. Claiming descent from the great Visigothic King Witiza Egiciz, Vitiza's heritage is one of myths and legends.

### East Francia

- Ludwig II 'the German' Karling, King of East Francia: An ambitious man, Ludwig has long sought to reconquer the pieces of the Empire he considers his birthright. These ambitions have suffered from setback after setback, however, as his various military campaigns all ended in failure and his rebellious sons have pried away most of his own lands from his control.
- Karlmann Karling, Duke of Bavaria: The eldest son of Ludwig the German, Karlmann rebelled against his father and forced him to give him control over the lands of Bavaria. He will expand this domain in wars with Slavs to the east, and later on will even be elected King of Italy — though an unfortunate stroke will force him to abdicate the thrones of Bavaria and Italy to his younger brothers.
- Karl Karling, Duke of Swabia: The youngest son of Ludwig the German, Karl would historically, through sheer luck, inherit all of his relatives' kingdoms that composed their massive Empire, but his luck ran out when he was deposed in 887, and he died the next year.
- Werner Salian, Count of Worms: Try to climb insurmountable odds and establish your house as masters of the Holy Roman Empire. The Salian dynasty provided four Holy Roman Emperors, an immense task to be sure!
- Liutbert von Babenberg, Count of Kärnten: Maybe someone could prevent the Babenberg dynasty extinction that would happen in 400 years?

### England

- Ælla Oswulfson of Northumbria, Petty King (Duke) of Northumbria: Having invited the wrath of the Great Heathen Army on not only yourself but the other kingdoms of England too, it'll take a lot more than a snake pit simply to save yourself from being made into a blood eagle. Can you call the bluff of these glorified bandits and throw them back across the sea, or will the ravening pagans have their vengeance?
- Alfred Æthelwulfson Wessex, Earl (Count) of Dorset: As the Vikings push further south, Earl Alfred of Dorset is readying his troops. In the coming years, Alfred will fight numerous battles against the Norsemen and put an end to the invasion. When he inherits his brother's throne, he becomes one of the greatest English kings throughout history.
- Burghred Beornredson of Mercia, Petty King (Duke) of Mercia: Burghred of Mercia rules as the last truly independent King of Mercia, a once-mighty Anglo-Saxon kingdom now caught between the growing power of Wessex and the looming threat of Viking invasion. As the brother-in-law of King Alfred of Wessex, Burghred once held sway over much of central England and maintained influence through his royal alliances.
- Eadmund Æthelweardson of East Anglia, Petty King (Duke) of East Anglia: Eadmund of East Anglia rules one of the last bastions of Anglo-Saxon power in eastern England. At war with the Great Heathen Army, Eadmund would later be remembered as Saint Edmund, martyred in 869 after refusing to renounce his Christian faith while in the hands of the Norse invaders.
- Dumnarth Cerneu, Petty King (Duke) of Cornwall: The last recorded king of Cornwall, Dumnarth is a remnant of independent Brythonic rule in England, ruling over a small duchy next to the petty kingdom of Wessex. Can you outmaneuver your Anglo-Saxon neighbors to restore the borders of the ancient kingdom of Dumnonia under the banner of Cornwall, or will your lands become new holdings for Alfred the Great?

### Croatia

- Braslav Vladinić Senulatović, Ban (Duke) of Slavonia: Braslav Senulatović, Duke of Slavonia, rules a crucial frontier between the Frankish Empire and the Slavic world. Historically, Braslav would become a key ally of Arnulf of Carinthia, future Emperor of the Franks, and fight alongside him against Great Moravia, before being tasked with defending Pannonia from the rising threat of the Magyars.
- Domagoj Dobimirić Domagojević, Ban (Duke) of Croatia: Domagoj Domagojević, Duke of Croatia, rose to power in 864 by seizing the throne from the Trpimirović dynasty. His reign was marked by fierce conflict with Venice, sparked by Croatian piracy in the Adriatic Sea and the growing independence of the Venetian Republic. Domagoj later supported King Louis II of Italy in his campaign against the Arabs in Bari.
- Zdeslav Trpimirić Tripimirović, Count of Požega : Zdeslav Trpimirović, Count of Požega, is the exiled son of Trpimir I of the Trpimirović dynasty. After his father's death in 864, Zdeslav and his brothers were exiled from Croatia by Domagoj Domagojević, who seized power.

### Great Moravia

- Rostislav Mojmírid, King of Great Moravia: Rostislav begins his reign having secured partial independence from the Franks. A few years before the 867 start date, he invited the famous Saints Cyril and Methodius to Moravia, introducing the Slavonic liturgy and the Glagolitic script to strengthen the kingdom’s cultural identity and loosen Frankish ecclesiastical control.
- Velemír Divinsky, Count of Orava: Velemír Divinsky, Count of Orava, rules over a strategic frontier in the eastern reaches of Great Moravia, serving under its future king, Svatopluk I, Duke of Nitra and future King of Great Moravia. Situated in the Carpathian highlands, Orava borders both pagan and Christian realms, offering countless opportunities for glory and conquest.

### Hungary

- Kocel, High Chieftain (Duke) of Balaton: Kocel was the second son of Pribina, a Slovien ruler who was attacked and expelled from his homeland by Mojmir I, duke of Moravia, in the early 800s. Kocel built a large fortress as his seat of power in the region of Lake Balaton, surrounded by forests and swamps along the river Zala, where he ruled over Sloviens in the area.
- Gostomil Bijelahrvatskić, High Chieftain (Duke) of Ungvár: Gostomil Bijelahrvatskić rules over the mountainous homeland of the White Croats, an elusive and ancient Slavic people of the Carpathians. Though little is known about them, they are believed to share a common ancestry with the southern Croats, having remained in the north instead of migrating to the Balkans.

### Italy

- Louis II 'the Younger' Karling, King of Italy: As the eldest son of Lothaire of Middle Francia (himself the eldest son of Kaiser Louis the Pious), Louis the Younger is the rightful successor to the entire Frankish Empire. However, imperial power had diminished so much by the time Louis was crowned Holy Roman Emperor that the title no longer offered any real authority.
- Lambert Guideschi, Duke of Spoleto: Since the fall of the Roman Empire, Italy has been ruled by a succession of barbarians from the north. Your ancestors may have been among them, but you are thoroughly Italian. You are one of the most powerful of the weak king's vassals, and while he may be able to call in his powerful family if you rise against him, you can fall back on your fellow vassals who are just as hostile...
- Boson Bosonid, Count of Provence: Boson Bosonid of Provence, is a lesser-known scion of the powerful Bosonid dynasty—founders of the Kingdom of Burgundy. His relative, Boson Bouvinid (Bivinids), would become its first king, and his son Louis, Burgundy's second monarch, would go on to claim the imperial title of Charlemagne himself. As one of the lesser-known sons of Boso the Elder, you are nonetheless well positioned.
- Konrad Welfen, Duke of Upper Burgundy: Konrad of Upper Burgundy, a member of the Bavarian branch of the Welf dynasty, played a significant role in Carolingian politics, earning many titles across Burgundy. Historically, his descendants would rise to become kings of Upper Burgundy, eventually uniting their realm with Lower Burgundy into a powerful and lasting kingdom.
- Theophylact Tusculani, Count of Orvieto: Theophylact of Orvieto stands as the founder of the Tusculani dynasty, a powerful Roman noble family that would go on to dominate papal and Italian politics for over two centuries. The Tusculani rose to prominence particularly during the Tusculan Papacy (1012–1048), a period in which they produced multiple Popes and held vast secular power in and around Rome.

### Sicily

- Adelchis Radelchisidi, Duke of Benevento: Adelchis Radelchisidi, Duke of Benevento, rules over one of the last independent Lombard duchies in Italy. Surrounded by hostile powers, the Kingdom of Italy to the north, the Byzantine Empire to the south, and Saracen raiders along the coast, Adelchis stands in a precarious position.
- Guaifer Dauferidi, Duke of Salerno: Guaifer Dauferidi, Duke of Salerno, rules a small but strategically important Lombard principality in southern Italy. A member of the powerful Dauferidi family, Guaifer rose to power by deposing his predecessor, Prince Adhemar, and exiling his nephew Daufer to secure his position. These bold moves earned him the support of both the nobility and the people of Salerno.

### Lotharingia

- Lothaire II Karling, King of Lotharingia: Though he was in love with his mistress Waldrada, Lothaire's father forced him into a political marriage with Teutberga. Their union produced no children, and Lothaire's life has been a continuous struggle to divorce his wife and get his bastard children legitimized. Though he never stops trying, Lothaire will go on to die without a legal heir.
- Guilhèm de Forez, Count of Lyon: Guilhèm begins his journey as a vassal of Lotharingia, ruling Lyon and Forez, lands his family would historically rule for close to 200 years. Here however he is in a good position to claim the Duchy of Viennois, at the moment held by his liege, Lothaire II of Lotharingia, laying the foundation for further conquests.
- Eberhard Etichonen, Count of Strassburg: Eberhard Etichonen, Count of Strassburg, stands at the heart of Europe—Alsace, a land of wealth and power, long contested by kings and emperors. As the grandfather of Guntram the Rich, whose descendants would one day found the House of Habsburg, Eberhard has the chance to secure dynastic glory not for the Habsburgs, but for the Etichonids themselves.
- Eudes Ardoinici, Count of Besancon: Eudes Ardoinici rules over the County of Besancon and its surroundings. Historically, the Ardoinici would migrate south into Italy, where they ruled the region of Turin and played a major role in the politics of France and the Holy Roman Empire.
- Rainier de Chaumontois, Count of Luxembourg: Rainier de Chaumontois serves as Count of Luxembourg and is the supposed grandfather of Wigeric of Lotharingia, whose descendants would go on to rule Luxembourg and the duchies of Lower and Upper Lorraine. As a distant member of the Karling dynasty, Rainier belongs to a lineage of legendary rulers.

### Navarra

- Gartzia Enekez Íñiga, King of Navarra: Gartzia faces a difficult position, with his son and heir, Orti Gartzies, held captive at the Umayyad court in Cordoba since 860. Historically, Gartzia would die in 870, likely during a rebellion against the Umayyads, leaving Orti to rule under the Sultan’s permission.
- Azeari II Garindez Galinda, Count of Aragón: Azeari II of Aragón, stands as a Basque noble of northern Iberia. Through his marriage with the daughter of King Gartzia Íñiga of Navarra, his children hold strong claims to the Basque throne. Azeari's father, Garindo Azearez of Aragón, played a notable role in early 9th century Basque and West Frankish politics, earning titles, prestige in the Kingdom of Navarra.

### Wales

- Rhodri II 'the Great' Aberffraw, Prince (Duke) of Gwynedd: At first glance Rhodri doesn't seem to be anything special, just another Welsh prince. But who knows, maybe you will be able to make him great? Perhaps surprisingly, he is already known as the Great...
- Meurig ab Arthfael Morgannwg, Lord (Count) of Gwent: Meurig Morgannwg, Count of Gwent, rules a proud land steeped in Roman and Arthurian legacy. His dynasty traces its lineage back to Emperor Magnus Maximus (Macsen Wledig), a Roman general who briefly ruled the Western Empire in the 4th century.
- Elisedd II ap Tewdwr Brycheiniog, Lord (Count) of Brycheiniog: Elisedd of Brycheiniog rules over one of the smaller Welsh kingdoms nestled in the hills of southern Wales. His dynasty traces its lineage to Irish raiders who settled in the region centuries earlier, giving the realm a unique blend of Irish and Welsh heritage.

### West Francia

- Bernat II Gellones, Duke of Gothia (Barcelona): Bernat revolted against the King of Aquitaine and got himself excommunicated by Pope John VIII in 878. Will you stay within the French kingdoms and perhaps become King yourself? Or will you adopt the culture of your subjects, declare independence, join the Iberian Struggle and return Barcelona to Iberia by forming the Kingdom of Aragon?
- Bernard Gellones, Count of Auvergne: Bernard Gellones, also known as Plantevelue or Plantapilosa in Occitan, is the son of Bernat I of Toulouse and Duoda of Gascogne, daughter of Antso I of Gascogne. Through his powerful lineage and his marriage to Ermengarda of Auvergne, Bernard holds claims on Toulouse, Gothia, Gascogne, and the rest of Auvergne.
- Charles II 'the Bald' Karling, King of West Francia: At the time of Charles's birth, his fully grown half-brothers already ruled their own kingdoms within the Carolingian Empire. Without a crown of his own, he became known as the Bald, though he eventually did inherit Aquitaine and West Francia.
- Louis 'the Stammerer' Karling, Count of Bellac: Louis the Stammerer was famous, and named so, for his physical weaknesses. He was crowned twice, the second time by Pope John VIII, after succeeding his father's rule of West Francia. Three of his sons would become Kings of West Francia, the last known as Charles the Simple, with a reputation of being straightforward.
- Eudes Robertine, Count of Anjou: In July of 866, the Viking raider Hæsteinn slew Eudes' father, Robert the Strong. Mere months later, his mother Adelais died giving birth to his brother Robert. The world is cruel and the future might seem uncertain, but these orphan boys are destined to sit on the French throne. Their descendants, the Capets, will rule France for centuries to come.

### Brittany

- Salomon mab Riwallon de Poher, King of Brittany: Salomon de Poher reigns as King of Brittany during a century of chaos and turmoil. Following treaties with King Charles the Bald of West Francia, Brittany stands loyal to the Franks in name, but remains effectively independent. Salomon would rule until his assassination by Breton nobles in 874, ending his ambitions for a stable and powerful realm.
- Pascueten mab Nominoë de Vannes, Count of Vannes: Pascueten de Vannes, a powerful Breton noble, courtier of King Salomon and relative of the legendary King Nominoë, stands as a claimant to the Breton throne. Pascueten would eventually orchestrate the assassination of King Salomon in 874, only to fall into a bloody struggle against his co-conspirator, Guruant de Rennes.
- Guruant mab Nominoë de Rennes, Count of Rennes: Guruant de Rennes, a Breton noble and relative of the famed King Nominoë is one of the main conspirators of the assassination of King Salomon of Brittany in 874. A claimant to the throne, he immediately turned on his co-conspirator, Pascueten de Vannes, igniting a bloody conflict for control of Brittany during which both men met their end.
- Alfrond mab Gestin de Cornouaille, Count of Cornouaille: Afrond de Cornouaille, a proud Breton noble, rules the lands of western Brittany during a time of rising chaos. With the end of King Salomon's rule approaching and the increasing Viking threat on the horizon the ancient lands of Brittany stand vulnerable to foreign invaders and internal strife.

### Alba

- Caustantín II MacCináed Ailpin, King of Alba: Caustantín Ailpín, King of Alba, stands at the head of a young and fragile kingdom forged from the unification of the Gaels of Dál Riata and the Picts of Pictland. Son of the famed Cináed MacAlpín, Caustantín is tasked with securing his father's legacy and defending Alba against Norse incursions and internal unrest.
- Crínán Crinan, Mormaer (Count) of Buchan: Crínán Crinan is a minor Gaelic tribal ruler in northern Alba, ruling over the region of Buchan. Though Gaelic influence is growing across the land, his domain remains home to many Picts, an ancient and once-mighty warrior people whose culture is rapidly fading in the face of Gaelic dominance.
- Artgal map Dumnagual Alt Clut, Petty King (Duke) of Strathclyde: Artgal Alt Clut, ruler of the Brythonic realm of Strathclyde, governs the last stronghold of Cumbrian culture in northern Britain. His realm lies precariously between the threats of Norse invasion, the rising power of the Gaelic kingdom of Alba, and the ambitions of the Anglo-Saxon realm of Northumbria.

### Iceland

- Auðr the Deep-Minded Flatnefr, Chieftess (Countess) of Vestrland: Auðr the Deep-Minded, daughter of Ketill Flatnefr, married Olaf the White, a Viking who declared himself King of Dublin. After Olaf's death in battle, she led her family on a daring voyage across the northern seas, passing through Orkney and the Faroe Islands before finally settling in Iceland.

### Byzantine Empire

- Basileios Makedon, Basileus (Emperor) of the Byzantine Empire: Basileios (Basil) the Macedonian, founder of the illustrious Macedonian dynasty that ruled the Empire for nearly 200 years, rose from humble origins to the pinnacle of power.
- Leon Phokas, Count of Seleukia: You are the founder of the Phokas family and great-grandfather of Basileus Nikephoros II, who was a brilliant military leader also known as the Pale Death of the Saracens. Build up your power base in Anatolia and eventually seize the imperial throne long before your dynasty historically would.
- Bagrat Bagrationi, Count of Kakheti: Head of the Bagrationi dynasty, your great grandson Adarnase IV of Iberia would go on to declare himself King of the Iberians establishing an independent Georgian kingdom for the first time in centuries.
- Demetre II Anchabadze, Prince (Duke) of Abkhazia: Demetre reigns over Abkhazia, a vital Christian stronghold at the crossroads of the Caucasus. Historically, he fortified the Anchabadze dynasty, paving the way for its influence under his successors, such as his brother and heir Giorgi, who steered Abkhazia toward prominence.
- Ioannes Aplakes, Strategos (Duke) of Strymon: Ioannes Aplakes, governor of Strymon and Thessalonika, commands one of the Byzantine Empire’s most vital frontier posts. Tasked with defending the empire’s northern border against the growing power of the Bulgarians, Ioannes stands at the crossroads of danger and opportunity. Historically, little is known about Ioannes beyond his crucial role as military governor.

### Sicily

- Gregorios Spartenos, Count of Napoli: You are an aging Greek Orthodox count with one province. You've got Lombards, Franks, and Muslims to deal with, most of them more powerful than you, to say nothing of the fact that most of your sons are celibate. The Pope (and Rome) is just two provinces away, and things are looking dire.
