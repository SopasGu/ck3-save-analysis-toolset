## Mechanics Notes

### Overview

- Laws are royal decrees that have a vital effect on the development of the realm. Decreasing the level of a law is free and can be done at any point but increasing the level of a law costs [[mechanics-content/resources|Prestige]].

### Borders Wars Policy

- The Border [[mechanics-content/warfare|Wars]] Policy law dictates if the liege will join its vassals' defensive wars against foreign rulers. They law is set automatically and cannot be changed.
- If the [[mechanics-content/titles|title]] rank is Hegemony, realm size is 160 or higher, the country is in a civil [[mechanics-content/warfare|war]], or House Unity is
- Antagonistic, the law will be set to Conditional Join. The liege will not automatically join its vassals' defensive wars but if the [[mechanics-content/warfare|war]] lasts 3 years they can use the Protect Vassal interaction to do so. Certain factions will also gain Discontent twice faster.
- If the [[mechanics-content/titles|title]] rank is not Hegemony, realm size is is less than 160, the country is not in a civil [[mechanics-content/warfare|war]] and House Unity is not
- Antagonistic, the law will be set to Guaranteed Protection. The liege will automatically join its vassals' defensive [[mechanics-content/warfare|wars]].

### Administrative laws

- Administrative [[mechanics-content/government|governments]] have access to the following laws. Changing them costs 200 [[mechanics-content/resources|Prestige]], increased by 1 per Realm Size and reduced by 1 per [[mechanics-content/attributes|Learning]]. Provincial [[mechanics-content/warfare|War]] Declaration law dictates which governors can wage external wars and requires the Imperial Bureaucracy law to be at the same level or higher.
- At level 1 governors can declare external [[mechanics-content/warfare|wars]]
- Frontier and Naval governors can declare external [[mechanics-content/warfare|wars]]
- Frontier and Naval governors can declare external [[mechanics-content/warfare|wars]] and require permission from the emperor
- At level 4 governors cannot declare external [[mechanics-content/warfare|wars]]

### Celestial laws

- Independent rulers with Celestial [[mechanics-content/government|government]] have access to the following laws. Changing them costs 200 [[mechanics-content/resources|Prestige]], increased by 1 per Realm Size and reduced by 1 per [[mechanics-content/attributes|Learning]]. Retirement Age law dictates the retirement age of governors. Can be set to 50, 60, 70, 80 or 90.

### Hegemony of China laws

- The Hegemony of China unlocks two additional laws as long as its [[mechanics-content/government|government]] remains Celestial. General Secretariat Control law dictates which councilor will be the Grand Secretariat diarch. Can be set to Grand Chancellor, Grand Marshal or Imperial Censor.

### Imperial Policy

- Independent emperors with Ritsuryō or Sōryō [[mechanics-content/government|government]] have access to the Imperial Policy law, which affects both themselves and their vassals. Changing the law 500 [[mechanics-content/resources|Prestige]]. Vassals can change the law by forming an Imperial Policy faction and getting the liege to accept its ultimatum or win the civil [[mechanics-content/warfare|war]].

### Coronation

- As long as the Coronation Laws game rule has not been set to No Coronation Laws, every King and above will have the Coronation law. Unlike other laws, it cannot be changed manually and is instead increased automatically upon completing a Coronation [[mechanics-content/activity|activity]].
