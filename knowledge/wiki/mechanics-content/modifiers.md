- A modifier is an effect that adds various bonuses or penalties to a [[mechanics-content/dynasty|dynasty]], house, character or [[mechanics-content/county|county]]. Modifiers can be temporary or permanent. Characters can have an unlimited number of modifiers but only the first 10 will be displayed.

### Personal Deity

- Personal Deity are modifiers obtained via the Determine Bhakti or Determine Personal Deity [[mechanics-content/decisions|decisions]]. Each character can choose from 4 modifiers depending on their [[mechanics-content/faith|faith]] and can replace them at any point by taking the decision again. The modifiers are lost if a character converts to a different faith.

### Honorary titles

### Warhorse

- The warhorse is a unique modifier that can gradually increase in effects. It can be obtained as a result of [[mechanics-content/travel|travel]], [[mechanics-content/attributes|Martial]] [[mechanics-content/lifestyle|Lifestyle]] or Wandering Lifestyle events. If the [[mechanics-content/royal-court|Royal Court]] DLC is installed it can also be obtained from royal [[mechanics-content/court|court]] events. The modifier will initially grant the following bonuses:
- The day after the modifier is obtained an event will trigger to give the warhorse a name. The modifier can be improved in two ways, both costing a small amount of [[mechanics-content/resources|Gold]] and offering a choice of what horse talent to improve. The modifier can only be improved twice.
- Every 275-400 days there is a 15% chance to gain the option to train the warhorse. If the Master of the Horse [[mechanics-content/court|court]] position is being used its holder can be asked to do it instead, which removes the [[mechanics-content/resources|Gold]] cost and grants a temporary +15 Opinion with the Master of the Horse but chooses the training at random. The event can only take place once.
- 25-30 years after obtaining the modifier the warhorse will start to die of old age and offer the option to breed it. Not breeding it will result in the loss of the modifier in 30-60 days.

### Pets

- Pets are character modifiers that last between 11 and 16 years. They can be inherited if a character dies before the pet but can also be lost through events. Both pets unlock a [[mechanics-content/decisions|Decision]] to pet them, have a hidden 33% chance to save the character when a Murder Scheme is executed against them, and have a very small chance to expose [[mechanics-content/schemes|schemes]] targeting the character. There are three pet modifiers:
- cat Pet Cat grants +1 to [[mechanics-content/attributes|Intrigue]] and Learning
- dog Pet Dog grants +1 to Diplomacy and [[mechanics-content/attributes|Prowess]] and can receive additional events during Hunt [[mechanics-content/activity|activities]]
- Hunting Eagle grants +1 to [[mechanics-content/attributes|Martial]] and Stewardship and can receive additional events during Hunt [[mechanics-content/activity|activities]] Pets can be obtained in multiple ways. One day after getting the pet the character will be given the option to input a name for the pet.
- At any point the character may be given a pet from a consort, friend, lover or vassal and gain the option to keep it or sell it for a minor random amount of [[mechanics-content/resources|Gold]].
