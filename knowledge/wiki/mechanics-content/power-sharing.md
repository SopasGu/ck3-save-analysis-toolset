## Mechanics Notes

### Overview

- Power sharing occurs when a significant amount of power is delegated to a second character, called the diarch, who helps their liege run their realm but may also use the power for their own gain. Being in a diarchy prevents the [[mechanics-content/titles|titles]] owner from changing their succession [[mechanics-content/laws|law]]. A ruler cannot imprison, revoke titles, retract vassals or ask for the conversion of their diarch.

### Mandates

- During the power sharing, the diarch will be tasked with a mandate to pursue while the liege is unavailable. There are three possible mandates and their effects: The diarch will receive events related to these mandates and be given the option to grant the benefit to their liege, split rewards, pocket it for themselves, or just ignore their duties entirely.

### Loyalty

- Each diarch has a loyalty rating, which determines how likely they are to abuse their powers. Selfless diarchs will not use borrowed powers and always give the mandate benefits to the liege. If the liege tries to end the power sharing they will always agree. Situational diarchs will sometimes use borrowed powers and always split the mandate benefits with the liege or ignore their duties.

### Scales of Power ​

- The Scales of Power go from 0 to 100 and represents the balance of the power shared between liege and diarch. The longer the power sharing lasts the more it increases towards the diarch, giving them more power in the realm. Barring other factors, Scales of Power tend to drift to 50. ### Regency

- Regency occurs for a ruler who is imprisoned by someone who is not their vassal or away on an [[mechanics-content/activity|activity]] taking place outside their realm. If the Scales of Power are at level 1 or the diarch has Selfless loyalty the Regency will end automatically when the ruler returns. Otherwise it requires using [[mechanics-content/resources|Prestige]], Piety, Gold or offering a Weak [[mechanics-content/hooks|Hook]] to the diarch.
- Regency has 4 levels. Each level grants the diarch unique interactions called borrowed powers. Borrowed powers can be vetoed by the liege by spending [[mechanics-content/resources|Prestige]], and using Borrowed powers may cause Strife - an Opinion penalty with all of the liege's vassals. ### Entrenched Regency and Co-Monarchy

- Entrenched Regents and Co-Monarchs have similar powers at the steps on the scale. However, there are some key differences.
- Co-Monarchy is started when a Feudal, Sōryō, Tribal or Wanua King or Emperor chooses to appoint a family member as co-monarch, in which case the diarch is also automatically appointed as designated heir. It can only be ended by imprisoning and executing the co-monarch.
- Entrenched Regency is automatic for an underage or Incapable ruler or one who takes the Enter Seclusion [[mechanics-content/decisions|decision]]. A level 4 Regency can also be turned into an Entrenched Regency. If a ruler comes of age the new balance for the Scales of Power is at 0 and the ruler can attempt to end the Entrenched Regency.

### Vizierate

- Vizierate occurs when a clan ruler appoints a vizier, which costs 350 [[mechanics-content/resources|Prestige]]. The diarch gains access to the Siphon Treasury borrowed power while the liege gains access to the Mulct Vizier interaction with the diarch, gaining gold and swinging Scales of Power towards the diarch. If the diarch is also a Tax Collector then it gains 50% of the current Scales of Power as Tax Collector Aptitude.

### Duumvirate

- Duumvirate is a form of power sharing only available to Emperors whose [[mechanics-content/culture|culture]] has the Roman Ceremonies [[mechanics-content/traditions|tradition]], and only close family members of the ruler or house heads of powerful families can be appointed as diarchs. The liege gets a percentage Influence bonus while the diarch gets a flat monthly bonus, which both increase every 5 years of power sharing, including those with a Nominal Duumvirate.

### Nominal Duumvirate

- Children can be given a Nominal Duumvirate instead. Upon becoming adult the power sharing will be turned into a regular Duumvirate, the diarch receives additional skill points depending on the Scales of Power swing and the Scales of Power swing is reduced by half. Both characters get the same Influence bonus as from a regular Duumvirate.

### Grand Secretariat

- Grand Secretariat is a form of power sharing used in Hegemony of China and cannot be ended. The diarch will be a councilor decided by the Grand Secretariat Control [[mechanics-content/laws|law]] and receives +1 Disciple Limit and +100 Movement Power. Grand Secretariats use a different set of mandates: Under Grand Secretariat the Scales of Power go up to level 6.
