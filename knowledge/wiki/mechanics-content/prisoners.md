## Mechanics Notes

### Overview

- Prisoners are characters who are held at someone's court unwillingly. A character can obtain prisoners via the Abduction scheme, occupying a character's realm capital while at war or following battles if the enemy commander has low Prowess. A ruler can attempt to imprison any character within their realm but this will incur tyranny, particularly if the prisoner is Noble, unless they are guilty of a Crime.

### Prisoner value

- A prisoner's title rank affects how valuable they are if released, ransomed or punished.

### Imprisonment effects

- Characters will be imprisoned in either the dungeon or under house arrest and can be freely moved between the two. By default prisoners are held in the dungeon if Lowborn and house arrest if Noble. While imprisoned they suffer the following penalties: A prisoner will gain Stress while in prison and will eventually die if they have too much stress.

### Negotiate release

- A ruler can offer to release a prisoner in exchange for one or more of the following: Releasing a prisoner with no demands costs 10 Dread and gives 20 Opinion for 10 years with the prisoner. Releasing a character with no demands may give Legitimacy if their title rank is high enough.

### Ransom

- A ruler can ransom imprisoned characters, exchanging gold or a weak hook for the prisoner's freedom. The Ransom interaction is used on the prisoner, not the recipient. The character who is sent the ransom is decided in the following order:
- The prisoner themselves, if they are unlanded
- Prisoners and their direct liege may also offer to pay ransom to the jailer, with an additional option of using a hook on the jailer. Vassals and courtiers who have their ransom paid for by their liege gain +5 opinion for 1 year. When paying with gold, the ransom cost of a given prisoner depends on the ransom recipient's primary title rank as well as the prisoner's relation with the recipient.

### Punishment

- Punishing a character will may grant Dread depending on punishment and the prisoner's title rank

### Execution

- Executing an imprisoned character gives -20 Opinion with the victim's dynasty, -25 Opinion with the victim's liege, -30 Opinion with the victim's friends and lovers, -50 Opinion with the victim's family and spouses, and +2 Tyranny unless the prisoner is guilty of a very severe crime or follows a faith considered Hostile or Evil. There are 3 execution methods:
- Characters with the Cannibal trait or whose faith has the Ritual Cannibalism tenet can choose to devour the prisoner, which grants -35 Stress at the cost of a 20% chance to gain the Ill trait and will give the character the Cannibal trait if they don't already have it.
- Executioner can choose to make the execution public, which grants +15 Popular Opinion for 5 years in the realm capital and increase Control in all domain counties by +3 for each Aptitude level.
- Adventurers who have the Long Pig Intrigue lifestyle perk can choose to turn the prisoner into a random amount of Provisions.
- If the character has a royal court and the Arbitrary, Deceitful, Callous, Sadistic or Vengeful trait, executing a rival has a 25% chance to give the option to turn their skull into a pedestal item artifact. The offer is guaranteed if the prisoner was a nemesis. If the ruler does not have an Executioner it will lose -30 Opinion with the Personal Physician or Antiquarian.

### Torture

- Torturing a character will give the following:
- The torturer loses −100 Piety unless their faith has the Exaltation of Pain tenet or they have the Divine Retribution perk
- The torturer gains +100 Piety if their faith has the Exaltation of Pain tenet
- The torturer loses −10 Stress if they have the Sadistic trait
- The torturer gains +10 Stress if they have the Trusting trait

### Tradition punishments

- The following punishments are only available for adult characters if their culture has certain traditions:
- Castration is available to cultures with the Amharic Highlanders, Court Eunuchs, Byzantine Traditions or Palace Politics traditions. If the prisoner has below 12 years he will gain the Beardless Eunuch trait, otherwise it will gain the Eunuch trait.
- Blinding is available to cultures with the Byzantine Traditions, Merciful Blindings or Palace Politics tradition. The prisoner gains the Blind trait. Blinding gives -50 Opinion for 50 years with the prisoner and -20 Opinion for 20 years with their close family and spouses.
- Maiming is available only to characters in a Duumvirate and will end the power sharing. Can choose to give the prisoner the Blind, Disfigured, One-Legged or Maimed. Males can also be castrated. If the co-emperor does it, they take all of the emperor's titles except the family nobly family one.

### Sun trial

- Sun trial is a punishment available to faiths with the Sun Worship tenet and only towards prisoners who are guilty of a crime. The characters will gain 100 piety pagan Piety. The prisoner will either die or survive, and if they survive and have the same faith they will gain +3 Monthly Piety and +15 Same Faith Opinion for 15 years.

### Crime

- Crimes are actions that allow a character to imprison someone from their realm without incurring tyranny and may remove tyranny from certain punishments. ### Familial crimes

- Familial crimes are caused by certain actions taken against a character and allows any ruler who is a close family member of the victim to imprison the criminal character.

### Religious crimes

- If a character has an infamy trait that the faith considers a crime them all characters of the same faith will view them as a criminal.
