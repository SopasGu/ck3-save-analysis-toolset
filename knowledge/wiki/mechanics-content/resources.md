## Mechanics Notes

### Overview

- There are four important resources which are necessary for most actions in the game: gold, prestige, piety and renown. Each resource can be spent on different things and all of them can be spent or gained through events.

### Gold

- Gold is a measure of a character's liquid wealth reserves. Most rulers start the game with gold equal to 18 months of income. When a ruler dies or abdicates, all of their gold is passed onto their primary heir regardless of succession [[mechanics-content/laws|law]].

### Treasury

- Treasury is a second storage for Gold for Rulers with governor efficiency Administrative, Celestial, Meritocratic or Meritocratic Khanate [[mechanics-content/government|governments]]. 95% of income obtained from vassals will be added to the Treasury instead of Gold. Gold can be manually converted to Treasury. Every 10 Gold transferred gives +1 Stress if the character has the Greedy [[mechanics-content/traits|trait]] and -1 Stress if the character has the Generous trait.

### Debt

- Debt occurs when a character has negative gold or treasury and the penalties stack if the character has a debt of both resources. During debt, characters cannot declare [[mechanics-content/warfare|war]], their [[mechanics-content/county|counties]] can always acquire corruption, their [[mechanics-content/army#men-at-arms|men-at-arms]] cannot reinforce, and they can experience various negative events.

### Prestige

- Prestige represents the amount of political capital, social influence and goodwill a character is able make use of at any given time. [[mechanics-content/barony|Holding]] [[mechanics-content/titles|titles]], especially of higher rank, as well as the [[mechanics-content/attributes|diplomacy]] skill are the main continuous sources of prestige, but many events can generate it on occasion as well.

### Piety

- Piety is a measure of how virtuous a character is in the eye of others of their religion. It is gained primarily from religious relations, the character's [[mechanics-content/attributes|learning]] skill, and [[mechanics-content/traits|traits]] that its [[mechanics-content/faith|faith]] considers virtuous. Piety is spent when interacting with the church and declaring [[mechanics-content/warfare|holy wars]]. The icon displayed for representing piety depends on the character's religion.

### Influence

- Influence is a resource available only to characters with governor efficiency Administrative, Celestial, Meritocratic, Meritocratic Khanate or Ritsuryō [[mechanics-content/government|government]] and represents their political reach and authority. It is gained primarily by being a governor, being part of the [[mechanics-content/council|council]], upgrading an estate or being allied to the house head of a noble family, particularly the top liege.

### Provisions

- Provisions is a resource available only to [[mechanics-content/adventurer|adventurers]] and is spent when moving the camp. How many are expended depends on the size of the [[mechanics-content/army#men-at-arms|men-at-arms]], the terrain on the route, and what officers are hired, and they can also be lost as a result of [[mechanics-content/travel|travel]] danger events. They are primarily obtained as [[mechanics-content/adventurer#contracts|contract]] rewards, hunts, visiting [[mechanics-content/barony|holdings]], or the Gather Provisions [[mechanics-content/decisions|decision]].

### Herd

- Herd is a resource available only to characters with Nomadic [[mechanics-content/government|government]] and represents their flock of animals. It is used as a currency between nomads, serves as a gate for increasing Dominance, and a percentage of it is converted into Horde Riders. The character with the highest Herd will become the [[mechanics-content/culture#culture-head|culture head]] of its [[mechanics-content/culture|culture]]. Every 1000 Herd increases Gold income by 0.

### Barter Goods

- Barter Goods is a resource available only to characters with Wanua [[mechanics-content/government|government]] and represents island resources. It is used for army upkeep and [[mechanics-content/army|armies]] can convert it to Gold at a 2:1 ratio by being raised as barterers and [[mechanics-content/travel|traveling]] to another ruler's [[mechanics-content/county|counties]].
