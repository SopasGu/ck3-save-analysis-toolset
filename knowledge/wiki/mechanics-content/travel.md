## Mechanics Notes

### Overview

- Travel is used for characters on the road who must leave their home for a time. The system is used by any activity that does not take place in the same barony as the character, as well as vassals who make the decision to petition or pay homage to their liege. Adventurers also travel is also used while undertaking contracts or moving their camp.

### Mechanics

- Traveling has two important factors to keep track of:
- travel speed Speed determines how fast the caravan will move compared to armies. At 100% the caravan will move at the same speed as armies.
- Safety reduces the chance of travel danger Danger events.
- Once a destination is selected a travel route will be created going through the shortest number of baronies. Detours can be manually added in order to avoid dangerous baronies or visit specific points of interest. While traveling, various events may occur.

### Points of interest

- When a travel is planned the map will display all points of interest, which are baronies that contain something noteworthy. Visiting a point of interest will give additional bonuses, which stacks if the barony meets multiple point of interest criteria. The following baronies are points of interest:
- Capitals of a Kingdom will give experience to the lifestyle matching its court type. If it does not have a royal court it will give
- Capitals of an Empire will give triple experience to the lifestyle matching its court type. If it does not have a royal court it will give
- Capitals of a ruler with an Illustrious or Mythical legend will give
- Holy sites will give 100 Piety. If the character has the Wise Man/Woman trait it will also gain experience for it.

### Battles sites

- Battle sites are points of interest that will give Martial lifestyle experience and has a 20% chance to give a commander trait if the character does not have two or more. The following historical battle sites exist, provided the game is started after their historical year: New battle sites will appear following battles that meet at least 3 of the following criteria:
- One of the war leaders, their heir or their consort/concubine was killed
- One of the war leaders, their heir or their consort/concubine was captured
- Neither side has twice or more troops than the other
- 65% of one of the armies became Fatal Casualties

### Travel options

- Each character who travels may select up to two travel options to improve traveling. The Gold cost depends on the character's primary title rank. Having the Well-Prepared lifestyle perk reduces the cost by 25%.
